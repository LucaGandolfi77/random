

## Chapter 3: "A Slight Misunderstanding"  

The detention room smelled of stale chalk and regret, a corner of Ridgemont High where punishments accumulated like dust in corners no one wanted to touch. Chloe Sterling sat against the wall, her arms crossed, her usual confidence fraying at the edges. The teacher, Mr. Delgado, loomed over her, his expression a mix of disappointment and exhaustion. “You’ve been suspended for three days,” he said, his voice low but firm. “For what? For calling a classmate ‘ugly’ in front of everyone? For turning a simple disagreement into a spectacle?”  

Chloe’s jaw tightened. She had expected this. She had prepared for this. “It wasn’t just a joke,” she snapped, her voice sharp enough to cut. “She was *disgusting*. You don’t get to walk around here with that kind of… *态度* and not expect consequences.”  

Mr. Delgado sighed. “Consequences are meant to teach, Chloe. Not to make you feel powerful.”  

Before she could retort, Ethan Cole stepped forward. He was quiet, his usual reserved demeanor intact, but there was a quiet intensity in his gaze that made Chloe freeze. She hadn’t noticed him until now, hunched in the back of the room, his eyes fixed on her.  

“Mr. Delgado,” Ethan said, his tone calm, almost… *uncharacteristic* of him. “Maybe there’s another way.”  

The teacher glanced at him, then back to Chloe. “Ethan, what are you suggesting?”  

Ethan didn’t answer immediately. He looked at Chloe, really looked at her—the way she was slouching, the way her hands were clenched into fists, the way her face was a mask of defiance. Then he spoke. “She didn’t mean to hurt anyone. She’s just… she’s never had to deal with people who aren’t like her.”  

Chloe’s breath caught. She hadn’t expected him to say anything at all. She had expected him to stand there, maybe mutter something about her being dramatic, or worse—*pitying* her. But instead, he spoke as if he understood something she didn’t.  

“You think I’m pitying you?” Chloe snapped, standing up. “I’m not some helpless victim here. I’m the queen of this school. You think I need your *sympathy*?”  

Ethan tilted his head slightly. “No. I think you need to stop thinking you’re above it all. You don’t get to decide who’s ‘deserving’ of respect based on how they look. That’s not how the world works.”  

The room fell silent. Chloe’s heart pounded. She had never been called out like this before. Not by someone who wasn’t part of her clique. Not by someone who didn’t seem to care about her reputation. She wanted to laugh, to mock him, to throw something at him. But something in his voice stopped her.  

“Maybe,” she said finally, her voice quieter than she intended. “Maybe I don’t.”  

Ethan smiled faintly. “That’s a start.”  

But Chloe wasn’t done. She took a step back, her mind racing. *Pity. He’s pitying me.* She had heard that word before, usually from people who wanted something from her. From her ex, from her friends, from anyone who thought they could manipulate her. And here was Ethan, a quiet, unassuming boy, saying something that made her stomach twist.  

“You don’t get to tell me what to do,” she said, her voice laced with venom. “You don’t know me. You don’t know what I’ve gone through.”  

Ethan didn’t flinch. “I don’t need to. I just know that you’re not alone in this. And maybe… maybe you don’t have to be.”  

The words hit her like a punch. She wanted to scream, to run, to do anything to make him stop. But instead, she sank back into her chair, her mind a storm of contradictions. *Why does he bother?* she thought. *Why does he even care?*  

She had spent her entire life building a wall around herself, a shield to protect her from the world’s cruelty. Ethan had walked right through it, unscathed. And now, for some inexplicable reason, she was wondering if maybe… *maybe* he wasn’t entirely wrong.  

But she wouldn’t admit it. Not yet.  

Later that day, in the hallway between classes, Chloe found herself lingering near the entrance to the library, where Ethan was sitting by himself, flipping through a book. She had seen him there before—always quiet, always reading. But today, something about him felt different. Not in a bad way. In a… *curious* way.  

“You okay?” she asked, her voice cautious.  

Ethan looked up, surprised. “Yeah. Just thinking.”  

“About what?”  

He hesitated. “About you.”  

Chloe froze. “What?”  

“I mean… why did you defend me in detention? You don’t have to do that.”  

She laughed, a sharp, bitter sound. “Maybe I just didn’t want to be alone with you in there.”  

Ethan blinked. “Is that it?”  

“Maybe.”  

He smiled, a real smile this time, and Chloe felt something strange twist in her chest. It wasn’t the warmth she was used to from her friends, the kind that came with a smirk or a joke. This was different. Quieter. More… *honest*.  

Before she could say anything, the bell rang, and students began filing out of the library. Chloe hesitated, then walked away, her mind still tangled in the conversation.  

That night, she lay in her bed, staring at the ceiling. The events of the day replayed in her mind, but this time, she wasn’t just replaying them—she was *feeling* them. The way Ethan had looked at her, the way he had spoken, the way she had almost… *listened*.  

*Why does he make me feel like this?* she wondered. *Why can’t I just hate him like I used to?*  

She pulled out her phone and scrolled through her messages. Her friends had been texting her all day, asking why she’d been so quiet. She didn’t respond. Instead, she typed a single message to Ethan:  

*“Why did you defend me?”*  

She hit send, then immediately deleted it.  

*Stupid.* She thought. *You don’t need to explain yourself to him.*  

But hours later, she found herself back at his locker, her fingers hovering over her phone. She typed again, this time with more care:  

*“Why did you say what you did in detention?”*  

She sent it.  

Ethan replied almost immediately.  

*“Because I don’t like seeing people treated like they’re invisible.”*  

Chloe stared at the message, her heart racing. *Invisible?* That wasn’t a word she associated with herself. She was the queen of Ridgemont High. She was the one everyone looked to. But Ethan… Ethan saw her as something else.  

*Maybe I *am* invisible,* she thought. *To everyone but you.*  

She closed her phone, her fingers still trembling. For the first time in a long time, she didn’t feel like she had to be perfect. She didn’t have to be the cruel, calculating girl who ruled with a whip. She could be… *human*.  

But the thought terrified her.  

Because if she wasn’t perfect, if she wasn’t the girl who could crush others with a single word, what did that make her?  

She didn’t know.  

But for the first time, she wasn’t sure she wanted to find out.
