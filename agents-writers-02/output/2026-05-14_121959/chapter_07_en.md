# Chapter 7: The Kitchen of Ghosts

The kitchen hummed softly, a steady pulse beneath the silence that pulsed through the walls. It was here, in this forgotten corner of his home, that he found himself—alone, as if time itself had folded in on itself. Shadows stretched long across the countertops, mirroring the hollow space in his chest. He reached for the coffee pot, fingers brushing against the cool ceramic, and wondered if the universe had forgotten him too.

The air felt thick, charged with something unseen. He swallowed hard, forcing himself to breathe, to steady his thoughts. The silence wasn’t empty. It was waiting. It was listening.

A flicker of movement caught his eye—just for a heartbeat—a shadow darting behind the fridge. He froze, breath catching in his throat. Was it just a trick of the light? Or had something finally moved into place?

He took a step forward, eyes scanning the kitchen, searching for answers. But the silence persisted, unyielding. Every sound seemed amplified: the soft clink of a spoon against a plate, the distant tick of a clock, the echo of his own footsteps fading into a void. He was trapped in this moment, suspended between the past and the unknown.

A wave of unease washed over him. Who would have done this? And why? He clutched the edge of the counter, grounding himself in the present. The pain of absence gnawed at him, raw and unfiltered. He couldn’t shake the feeling that he was being watched—not by a person, but by something deeper, more ancient.

He took a deep breath, trying to center himself. “Just a moment,” he whispered, his voice trembling. “Just a moment.”

But the silence only deepened. The kitchen seemed to pulse with a slow, inevitable rhythm, as if waiting for something else to arrive. He felt the weight of it all pressing against his mind, tugging at the edges of his sanity.

With a shaky breath, he stepped back, leaving the empty space behind. The stillness wasn’t comforting; it was a reminder of what had been lost. He needed to return, to confront the emptiness head-on. But for now, he remained here, in the kitchen of ghosts, waiting for the next whisper.

As he poured the coffee, he paused, watching the liquid swirl in the vessel. It was not just a drink; it was a bridge between moments, a connection to the life he once knew. The warmth seeped into his hands, grounding him, yet also reminding him of how far he had fallen.

The silence pressed in around him, thick and suffocating. He closed his eyes, trying to forget the weight of it, but the shadows refused to leave. Each second stretched into an eternity, and in that moment, he realized how fragile the boundary between reality and memory had become.
