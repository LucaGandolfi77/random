## Chapter 10 – A Storm of Protests

The sky had once again turned a bruised violet, as if the clouds themselves were bruised by the gravity of what was to come. The city’s concrete arteries pulsed with an unspoken rhythm, a low hum that matched the beat of every citizen’s heart. For the first time in years, the usually placid silence was shattered by the thud of boots, the hiss of sirens, and the chant of a thousand voices rising in unison.

Mara stood at the edge of the plaza, her eyes scanning the crowd that had gathered like a living wave. Every face was a mask of resignation, but some flickers of defiance slithered beneath the surface. She could feel the heat of the protestist’s hands, the electric tremor of their words. The Ministry’s new “Love Regulation” had been announced a week ago, a blanket sentence that would enforce emotional isolation, a law that would strip citizens of the ability to feel, to touch, to whisper sweet nothings in the dark. It was a cold, sterile decree that made the city feel like a hospital waiting room.

Leo was a few meters away, his hair damp from the rain that had started to fall as the protest intensified. He was a fierce silhouette against the backdrop of protest signs that read, “Love is human. Love is real.” He had placed a torn fragment of a letter in his pocket—one that was once a whispered promise of a better tomorrow. He could feel the pulse in his own ears, like a drumbeat, and his fingers tightened around the paper as if he were protecting a fragile bird.

Mara had found herself in this sudden maelstrom, a place where the law forced everyone to look in the same direction, but hearts wanted to break the line. The Ministry’s drones hovered overhead like bloodied birds, their lenses gleaming. The city, which had been a gift of engineering and regulation, was about to taste the raw, unfiltered heat of humanity.

She felt a tremor of recognition in her chest. **This is the moment I have been waiting for.** Her mind went back to the first time she had heard Leo’s voice, the sound of laughter that had shaken her gallery of monotonous days. Leo had been a stranger, a maintenance worker who had never known love. He had been very careful with his words, but the moment had been one of truth, a line he had spoken that floated over the threshold of her guarded heart.

“Athena,” she whispered to herself, recalling the name from the old lullaby that had once been sung by mothers in the city’s abandoned gardens. The name had become a shroud of comfort, a reminder that love was not an abstract idea but a living, breathing being. As the protest grew louder, the chorus of chants sounded like the echo of a distant lullaby that spoke of a time when people embraced each other, their hearts beating in duet.

The protest swelled into a wave of emotion that threatened to sweep the city under its tide. The Ministry’s drones buzzed like bees, their cold metallic bodies cutting through the air. The people were not afraid; they were angry. They were driven by a hope that could not be contained. The storm that had risen above the city’s grey façade was a storm of collective hearts.

The protest seemed to have taken a life of its own as people shouted slogans about their right to feel, about the right to love. The city’s hum turned into a crescendo of human emotion. The Ministry’s drones pitched to silence the protests, but the people’s voices rose higher, filling the streets with unrestrained emotion.

Mara’s eyes tracked the movement of the crowd, and she could see that it was not just a group of people shouting slogans. It was a mosaic of people who had lost their warmth, their radiance, and their ability to feel the world. Their eyes were wider than the city’s streets, their faces of people who had been taught to hide the light within.

In the middle of the protest, Leo’s eyes met Mara’s. The world fell away, and they were the only two people in a city that had been turned into an iron cage. In that moment, the protest was a background, while the electric hum of the two lovers was the center of her universe. He looked at her as if she were the answer to his longing. He had never once felt this than this. The world’s laws were no longer enough to keep him from reaching for the one thing he truly desired: love.

He took a step forward, unhesitatingly. His hand was an open palm, a plea for Mara to understand. Mara’s heart went into overdrive, her chest feeling a strange, warm sensation. It was as if a small flame had lit, a spark that would ignite the darkness within her. The protest was still raging, the crowd still pushing. But the two lovers were in a different world—a world where emotion and longing were not regulated.

“Leo,” she whispered, her voice trembling. Her own voice felt like a breath of wind, as if the two of them were breathing within the same small space.

The air had changed, as if a new wind had arrived, a wind that came from the hearts of the people around them.
