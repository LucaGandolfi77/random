## Chapter 4 – A Glimpse of Light

The reef had grown familiar in the space between Maya’s fingertips and the water’s surface. She had mapped its folds, its glimmering alleys, and the hidden chambers that seemed to breathe with the rhythm of the currents. But it was the quiet pause—an unspoken invitation—that made the reef feel alive, as if it were a living entity awaiting her touch.

When she surfaced near the wreck of the *SS Calypso*, the gulls cried a high, piercing song, and a hush fell over the ocean. The wreck was a skeleton of steel and coral, like a cathedral swallowed by the sea. Its decks were a maze of twisted metal, where the seaweed crawled like ivy up the walls, and the sun’s rays, filtered through a broken hull, painted gold bars across the floor.

She swam through the wreck as if she were walking through a dream. Her breath was a steady rhythm, her heartbeat in sync with the sea. A sudden warmth wrapped around her like a secret candlelight. A flicker of silver—soft, luminous—pulled at her eyes. In the darkness of the wreck, a creature emerged from the shadows, its scales a mirror of the moonlit water.

It was Lira.

She did not realize at first that she was watching a creature; she thought she was seeing herself reflected in a surface. Lira’s presence was a quiet, unhurried glint. Her fins cut through the water with the same languid grace as a violinist's bow. Her eyes, pale and luminous, found Maya's. For a moment, the two were the same soul—two bodies bound by the same breath.

Maya felt a shiver run through her core. The ocean, a realm of mystery, had offered her a kind of companionship that was more intimate than any human bond. The creature’s translucent skin sang with a light that she had never seen before. It was as if a thousand tiny lanterns were chanting in unison, illuminating the depths.

“Hello…?” Maya whispered, not sure whether she was speaking to a god or to a living being. Her voice dissolved into the water, a soft vibration that carried across the wreck’s arches.

Lira's response was a pattern of pulsating glows, a gentle sequence that seemed to echo the rhythm of her breath. Maya followed the pulse, noticing the subtle shifts in color that danced across the creature’s belly. Warm amber, cool blues, and a hint of violet rolled together like a living spectrum.

Maya's hand extended, hesitant yet drawn by a magnetic pull. Lira, aware of the gesture, moved closer, her fins gliding in a reverent dance. The creature's translucent skin brushed gently against Maya's forearm, and she felt an electric thrill that sent a ripple through her entire being.

The moment felt like a promise—an unspoken agreement that the unknown could be made known. Maya closed her eyes, and in the silence that followed, she realized that light was not only a physical phenomenon but also an emotional one. She had always studied light in the context of biology and physics, but now she saw it as a dialogue between two beings.

She let her fingers glide over the creature's scales, feeling the smoothness that had been carved by the pressure of the ocean. Her thoughts drifted to the stories she had read about merfolk, of a world with swirling currents and hidden songs. The myths had sounded like fairy tales, but now they seemed closer than ever, a breath away from her.

The creature seemed to understand the depth of her wonder. It turned its head toward a part of the wreck she had never seen before—a chamber that seemed to whisper its secrets. Lira’s eyes shone brighter, and a spark of curiosity ignited within Maya. She followed the creature, guided by the luminous trail she left behind her.

The chamber was a cavern lit by bioluminescent algae, a waterfall of light that cascaded down the walls. The coral glowed in an ethereal palette that resembled twilight. The water seemed to hum with faint music, as if the reef itself were singing. Maya’s heart pounded—a rhythmic drum of anticipation and fear.

Inside the chamber, the gap in the wreck’s hull was wide enough for two to pass through. Lira guided Maya into the darkness, and together they entered a space that was a world unto itself. The depths were not dark but alive. The walls were lined with a cornice of shining shells that refracted the light, creating an internal aurora. A faint scent of salt and something sweet drifted through the water, a perfume that filled Maya’s senses with the scent of the sea.

Maya felt a sudden, inexplicable urge to cry. She let the tears flow down her cheeks, a watery blessing that stitched her soul into the deep. The tears mingled with the watery glow, and she felt her world become a living river of light. The moment she let herself be vulnerable, she could feel the currents speaking to her, telling her that the reef was alive, that it loved.

Lira's presence was a presence of grace. She stayed close, her pad of light providing a sense of safety. The creature’s calmness was a balm to Maya’s mind. Maya’s eyes filled with awe; she could not deny that her heart was beating in a more rhythmic cadence, as if it were a separate entity.

When Maya finally emerged from the chamber, she found herself on the deck of the *SS Calypso*—her thoughts as clear as the morning sky. The wreck was now a playground of light, a magical realm that was hers to explore. Maya felt a connection so deep that it seemed to dance between her and Lira, a silent vow that the ocean would never be the same again.

She drifted back to the wreck, asking herself what a creature like Lira could mean for her research. A glimmer of hope flickered in her mind: perhaps, in learning to communicate with her, she could unlock the secrets that lay hidden beneath the water. The journey was only beginning, but she felt ready to face whatever lay ahead.

The ocean had whispered a secret to her. She had heard it. And she was ready to listen to the words that the waves carried.
