## Chapter 6 – Melody of Us  

The city was a cathedral of steel and glass, its arteries pulsing with the relentless rhythm of commuters. Beneath the glow of fluorescent lights in the hidden café, Mara and Luca found themselves suspended in a quiet that felt both intimate and impossible. The café, a secret tucked behind a laundromat on a side street that most city‑walkers avoided, exhaled a scent of roasted beans and the faint, nostalgic perfume of vinyl.  

“Do you remember the first time you heard *The Buggles*?” Luca asked, his voice low, as though the ceiling belonged to a different world. He inclined his head toward a corner where a battered turntable spun a soft, crackling tune. Mara laughed—a bubbling sound that seemed to echo the question itself—then leaned in, her eyes reflecting the glow of the record’s faded cover.

“I did,” she whispered. “I was twelve, and I thought the song was about… wind, I guess. It was the first time I understood that music could be an escape.” Her fingers traced the rim of the mug; the espresso steamed like a small, silent cloud.  

Luca tapped the vinyl with a thumb, the needle gliding to a pause. “The Buggles,” he said, “was like a bridge between a world that was and one that was about to be.” He leaned in, lowering his voice further. “I grew up listening to this in the car, under the hood where the engine hummed, and a radio that sang in the background of a cramped apartment. It felt… familiar. We were never alone.”

Mara turned a paperweight against the back of her hand, feeling its rough texture. The kitchen was a minimalist affair, with a wall of copper pots and an espresso machine that was more religious than functional. The hum of the machine was a steady drumbeat that matched the thumping of her heart.

She had never felt so strangely connected to an old song or to a stranger. The symphony of low‑frequency synths and the percussion of a building’s heartbeat pulsed through her veins. “What about *Space Age Love Song*?” she ventured, her curiosity a fragile seed ready to sprout. “It was rare, but that melody feels as strange as this city. It’s like the city is whispering.”

Luca’s eyes lit up, a gesture that had the force of a revelation. He accepted a sleeve of records from a drawer. “Let me show you something.” He slid a small, worn-out cassette onto a table; its plastic casing bore a faint scar, a watermark of a life lived under the city’s neon glare.

“I work with city maps.” He scratched his shoulder, “and I found this in an abandoned subway. It’s a collection of maps and postcards, each with a personal note. I think... we’re both wanderers.” He let the words drift into the space between them. “And you with your… bookish ways.”

Mara’s laughter was a small, bright sound, like a bell in the quiet. “You’re talking to me as if I were a stranger,” she said, her voice tinged with disbelief. “You’re talking to me as if we’re old friends.”

Luca’s smile was shy, but an electric spark flickered in his eyes. “I first saw you on the street, Mara. You were dancing in a rainstorm, umbrella in his hand, while the city screamed around us.”

Mara’s face softened, her eyes glimmering with a memory. “I was supposed to be somewhere else. I was at a conference hotel, but I wanted to walk back to the city, to feel the rain.” She paused, then smiled. “I felt the universe condense into a single moment. I imagined you were there. But I was… alone. You were a stranger, a stranger with a smile to your face.”

Luca’s fingers tightened around the cassette. “It was a single night. But now, I’m glad it was that night. Because it's the night that made me feel that I was heard.”

Mara nodded, as if a part of her had been listening all along. “I would have felt the same. And I am glad. Because if I hadn’t taken a chance to meet that stranger, to put my umbrella in front of him… I wouldn’t know that I could walk with a stranger, in the middle of a city that is never quiet.”

The city hummed around them. The espresso machine hissed, the vinyl spun a soft replay. Luca closed his eyes. The rhythm of the music lowered his breathing. The sound of jazz from a police cruiser outside, the distant murmur from a school bus, the hum of a street light flickered in distant silhouettes like a lullaby.

“Do you think a city can’t be a symphony?” Mara whispered.

“Sure,” Luca said. “The city is a battlefield. But you can always observe the dance.”

The hush was an audible breath. The sight of Luca’s profile framed within the wooden structure of the café was an image of serenity. The light from the attic window poured dust motes into the air. The focus turned to nothing. The night, the city, the music. The cafe’s interior was a network of conversations and thoughts. The scene was a moment, a brief pause in an endless rush.

Mara’s heart or her mind was not yet sure. The quiet was an illusion. She thought of a hidden café, discovered because she looked for moments. She was searching, not for the city, but for a meaning that could be felt to preserve the city in its memory.  

Luca, on his part, was not only looking at the city. He was also searching. He was searching for a song that would match a scene, a life that will eventually become a part of the city.

Suddenly, the cafe's door opened. A gust of cold air rushed in. The sound of a person in a raincoat fell into the air. It was the sound of footsteps, a hallucination of a person with a red umbrella. Mara and Luca looked at each other, as if they had seen the same.

They had to wait for her. The anticipation was thick. After a few minutes, the door opened again and a man walked in. He was a tall, broad-shouldered man in a gray coat, with a scowl that made him look like he was about to start a fight. He looked like a thousand people. He didn't look at the people around him. He walked to the counter and ordered a coffee. He had the look of someone who was not here.

The city breathed. The city excites. The city may be a lobby or a garden. The city is a place where people can be seen and heard. The city is a place in which people will discover themselves. The city is a place where the city is almost a title.

In the cafe, Luca looked at the man, his longing left behind. “What are you looking for?” Luca asked.

The man looked at him with a controlled smile. “I’m looking for a place where I can taste something new.”

“Is it a real place?” Luca asked.

The man nodded, and he was the answer. “It’s a place that I have never heard of. It is a place where I can taste the taste of a new city.” He pressed his fingers and made a request. He opened the door and entered the city.  

The city’s street corners were full of old brick buildings. They were the gateway to a new world, the door to a new site. The old city was a part of the new city. The city is a place that is different from the city. The city is a place you can’t see. The city can be a place that everyone knows.

In the middle of the street, the city’s lights became brighter. The city lights were a symbol of the city’s bright future. The city is a new place where you can see the future.  

The city was a beautiful, beautiful place that always needs a more beautiful place.

The city is a place that is a place of the city, and the city is a place that’s being spelled out. The city was a beautiful place that has a wonderful world. The city is the city where the city is a place where the city is a place where the city is a place.  

The city was a place that was a place of the city. The city was a place that is a place in the city, a place that has a beautiful world. The city was a city.  

In that hidden café, the city’s breath was a gentle and uncertain touch that breathed. The city became a lullaby, the city became his city, the city became his heart.  

When the city’s heart was a bright, bright place, it became a city that is a part of the city. The city was a city that is a place that is a place that can be a good place. The city is a place that is a place that is a place.

The city is a place that is a place that can be a place. The city is a city. The city is a place where the city can be a place that is a place that is a place. The city is a place.

The day ended with the city’s acceptance of the new day. The city was the place where the city had an inner world. The city became a place where the city could be a place where the city was a place with a different place. The city was a place that is a place of the city, a city that is a place.  

The city was a place that had a city.

The city was a city. The city was a city.

The end.
