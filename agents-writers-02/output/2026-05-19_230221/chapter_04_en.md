
## Chapter 4: Searing Beginnings  

The skillet hissed as Alex tilted it, letting the olive oil swirl like liquid gold. Jamie leaned against the counter, arms crossed, watching the way Alex’s wrist flicked—confident, almost reckless. The scallops, still damp from their ice bath, hit the pan with a chorus of pops. Jamie’s jaw tightened.  

“They’re too wet,” they said, voice sharp. “You’ll steam them instead of searing.”  

Alex didn’t look up, but a smirk played on their lips. “Relax. I’ve got this.” The spatula clinked against the pan as they nudged a scallop, its edges curling into a perfect caramelized crust. “See? Golden already.”  

Jamie’s chest tightened. It *was* perfect—better than their own attempts last week. But pride prickled. They pushed off the counter, stepping closer. The heat from the stove brushed their face, mingling with the flush creeping up their neck.  

“You’re crowding the pan,” Jamie muttered, nodding at the scallops. “Let them breathe.”  

“Oh, I’m *crowding* them?” Alex arched a brow, finally turning. Their eyes were bright, daring. “Maybe I like a little chaos.” The spatula jabbed toward Jamie’s chest, playful but pointed. “Careful, Chef. Your OCD is showing.”  

Jamie’s laugh was half-irritation, half-relief. They reached for a towel to wipe their hands, then froze. Alex’s gaze dropped to their fingers, still dusted with flour from the onion prep. A beat passed. The kitchen seemed to shrink.  

*What are you doing?* Jamie thought, swallowing. *They’re just scallops. Just a cook. Just—*  

“You’re staring,” Alex said softly.  

“So are you.”  

The scallop in the pan sizzled, a reminder. Alex turned back to the stove, but not before Jamie caught the faintest tremor in their hand. Interesting.  

“They need basting,” Jamie said, grabbing a spoon. “Here.” They leaned in, close enough to feel the warmth radiating off Alex’s back. “Butter, garlic, thyme. Trust me.”  

Alex’s breath hitched—a tiny sound, but Jamie heard it. They stepped aside, making room. Jamie’s fingers grazed theirs as they reached for the butter. Both froze.  

“Sorry,” Jamie whispered.  

“Don’t be.”  

The spatula clanged against the pan as Alex flipped the scallops. Jamie watched, mesmerized, as the raw side met the heat. The smell was intoxicating—briny, nutty, with a hint of the herbs they’d tossed in earlier.  

“They’re ready,” Alex said, plating them with a finesse that made Jamie’s chest ache.  

“They’re… good.”  

“Better than your risotto last night?”  

Jamie snorted. “That was a disaster.”  

“Yeah, well, you’re not the only one who’s messed up a dish.” Alex’s smile was rueful. “Remember my soufflé? Collapsed like a deflated balloon.”  

“That was one time.”  

“And you never let me forget it.”  

The teasing tone was light, but Jamie heard the edge beneath. They’d sparred like this before—sharp words masking something softer. Tonight felt different, though. The air was thicker, charged like the moment before lightning strikes.  

Jamie crossed their arms again, avoiding Alex’s eyes. “You’re distracting me. I need to—”  

“What? Critique my technique?” Alex laughed, but it didn’t reach their eyes. They set the plate down, wiping their hands on a towel. “Come on, Jamie. We’re supposed to be a team.”  

*Team.* The word hung between them. Jamie’s pulse quickened. They opened their mouth to reply but stopped. The timer on the oven buzzed, shrill.  

“Pasta’s ready,” Alex said, moving toward the stove.  

Jamie let them go, but their gaze lingered on the way Alex’s sleeves rode up, revealing forearms dusted with flour. *Focus*, they scolded themselves. *This is work.*  

But the kitchen felt less like a workspace now and more like a stage, with just the two of them performing a dance neither had choreographed.  

As Alex drained the pasta, Jamie found themselves reaching for the pea purée they’d made earlier. The bright green swirl in the bowl reminded them of spring—new beginnings, maybe. They began plating, their movements deliberate, precise.  

“You’re overthinking,” Alex said, appearing at their side.  

“Says the person who nearly burned the scallops.”  

“They’re not burned. They’re… caramelized.”  

Jamie rolled their eyes but didn’t pull away when Alex’s hand brushed theirs again. This time, neither moved.  

The first course sat between them, a testament to their clashing styles—an elegant pea purée under Alex’s rustic scallops. Jamie’s chest swelled with something like pride.  

“It’s… actually good,” they admitted.  

Alex’s smile was soft now, almost shy. “We make a decent team, don’t we?”  

The words hung in the air, heavy with meaning. Jamie’s throat went dry. They nodded, unable to speak.  

Outside, the city buzzed on, oblivious to the quiet storm brewing in the kitchen. Inside, the heat from the stove and the space between them grew unbearable.  

Alex took a step back, breaking the spell. “Let’s get the risotto started.”  

Jamie exhaled, nodding. But as Alex turned away, they caught themselves smiling. The tension wasn’t gone—it had just shifted, simmering beneath the surface like a dish that knew its time was coming.  

And Jamie couldn’t wait to taste it.
