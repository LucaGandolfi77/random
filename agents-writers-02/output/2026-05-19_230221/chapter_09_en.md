
## Chapter 9: The Wine Spill

The bottle slipped from Alex's suddenly clumsy fingers, arcing through the air in slow motion before shattering against the edge of the counter. Dark liquid cascaded across the worn wooden surface, splashing onto Jamie's white shirt with the violence of a small explosion.

"Shit," Jamie breathed, frozen in place as the wine seeped into the fabric, spreading like a bruise across their chest. The rich, earthy scent of Cabernet filled the cramped kitchen, mingling with the lingering aromas of seared scallops and fresh herbs.

Alex's face flushed crimson as they grabbed napkins from the holder, their movements frantic. "I'm so sorry, I wasn't looking, I mean—the bottle just slipped and—"

"It's okay," Jamie said, though their voice carried an edge of disbelief. Wine stains were notoriously difficult to remove, and this particular vintage had cost more than their weekly grocery budget. They peeled the damp fabric away from their skin, watching the dark droplets continue to bloom across the cotton.

"Come here," Alex said softly, stepping closer. The space between them seemed to shrink, charged with something neither had quite acknowledged yet. Up close, Jamie could see the genuine distress in Alex's eyes, the way their lower lip trembled slightly as they reached out to touch the stained fabric.

"This might help," Alex murmured, dipping their fingers into the cold water from the tap and then pressing them gently against Jamie's chest. The coolness was shocking against Jamie's heated skin, but it was Alex's touch that sent shivers racing through them—not from the temperature, but from the intimacy of having someone so close, their face inches from Jamie's own.

The kitchen fell away. The timer beeping from the oven, the sound of cars passing outside, even the faint music still playing from the radio—all of it faded until there was only this: Alex's hands on their body, Alex's breath warm against their neck, Alex's eyes searching theirs with an intensity that made Jamie's knees weak.

"You're shaking," Alex observed, their voice barely above a whisper.

"So are you," Jamie replied, their own voice equally hushed.

The spell broke slightly when Alex pulled back just enough to look at them properly. In the dim light filtering through the window, Jamie could see the uncertainty there, the same wariness they felt themselves. They had been dancing around this moment for hours, maybe longer, each touch charged with meaning they pretended not to notice.

"Let me," Alex said, pressing harder with the damp napkin against Jamie's chest. The gesture should have been professional, practical even, but the way their fingers traced the line of Jamie's collarbone made it anything but clinical.

Jamie's breath hitched as Alex's hand slipped beneath the stained fabric, pressing directly against their skin. The cool water mixed with the lingering warmth of their body, creating a sensation that was simultaneously shocking and exhilarating. They could feel Alex's heartbeat against their chest, rapid and uneven, matching their own.

"We need to clean this properly," Alex said, though their hands didn't move away. Instead, they pressed closer, their other hand coming up to cup Jamie's face. Their thumbs brushed against Jamie's cheekbones with infinite gentleness, as if memorizing the shape of their face.

Jamie wanted to close their eyes, to lean into that touch and let themselves be consumed by it. They wanted to tilt their head just so, to give Alex better access to the pulse point beneath their jaw that Alex seemed particularly interested in. But more than anything, they wanted to know if Alex felt what they felt—that electric current running between them, that unspoken question hanging heavy in the air.

"You have a little mark here," Alex said, their voice rough with something that might have been longing. Their finger traced the edge of Jamie's jaw, then moved lower, stopping just above Jamie's lips. Close enough to kiss. So close Jamie could feel the warmth of their breath.

The moment stretched, taut as a wire ready to snap. Jamie could hear their own heartbeat pounding in their ears, could feel Alex's hesitation vibrating through both of them. Every instinct screamed for them to close the distance, to finally taste what they'd both been fantasizing about since that first near-miss in the market.

But Alex pulled back first.

"I should get you a new shirt," they said, stepping away so quickly they nearly tripped over their own feet. The disappointment that flooded Jamie's chest was almost physical, a tightening in their throat that made it hard to speak.

"Don't worry about it," Jamie managed, though they couldn't hide the waver in their voice. "It's just a shirt."

"No, I mean it. You can't serve dinner in a wine-stained shirt. That's... that's not right." Alex moved toward the bedroom area, their steps quick but purposeful, like someone fleeing from something they couldn't control.

Jamie watched them go, their stomach twisting with something that felt suspiciously like rejection. They told themselves they were being ridiculous—this was Alex, their friend and colleague, someone who had literally just touched them with more intimacy than anyone had in months. But the pull-away, the sudden retreat, felt personal somehow. Like Alex had seen something in that moment that made them reconsider whatever had been building between them.

The wine had stopped spreading, settling into the fabric with the finality of a bruise. Jamie peeled the shirt away from their skin again, examining the damage in the mirror above the sink. It was worse than they'd initially thought—a large, irregular stain that would be impossible to miss. 

They heard Alex moving around in the bedroom, opening drawers, searching through clothes. The sound was both comforting and frustrating, because it meant Alex was trying to help, but also that whatever moment they'd been on the verge of sharing was now firmly in the past.

"What size are you?" Alex called out, their voice muffled by the bedroom wall.

"Medium," Jamie replied, though they weren't entirely sure. Most of their clothes came from the same few stores, and they'd never really paid attention to sizes beyond whether things fit.

Footsteps approached, and Alex reappeared, holding a clean white button-down that looked almost identical to the stained one. "Will this work?"

Jamie nodded, taking the shirt with hands that were still slightly unsteady. As their fingers brushed, both of them froze for a brief second, remembering the intimacy of the previous minutes. But where Jamie might have lingered, Alex pulled back quickly, turning toward the kitchen to give them privacy.

"You can change in here," Alex said, nodding toward the small bathroom. "I'll... I'll finish up the appetizers."

The bathroom was barely large enough for the toilet, sink, and shower stall, but it offered the privacy Jamie needed. They closed the door behind them and leaned against it, closing their eyes and trying to steady their racing thoughts.

What the hell was that? They kept asking themselves as they peeled off the ruined shirt. Why had Alex pulled away? What had they seen, or felt, that made them retreat so quickly?

They slipped into the clean shirt, the fabric soft against their skin, and for a moment they just stood there, breathing deeply. The scent of wine still lingered in their hair, a reminder of how close they'd come to crossing a line that, until that moment, neither of them had been brave enough to approach.

When they emerged, Alex was carefully arranging the seared scallops on a platter, their movements precise and controlled. The earlier fluster was gone, replaced by the focused intensity that Jamie had come to recognize from their time working together in the restaurant kitchen.

"Everything okay?" Alex asked without looking up, but Jamie could hear the underlying question: Are we okay?

"Yeah," Jamie said, their voice steadier than they felt. "Just... thanks for the shirt."

"Don't mention it." Alex finally looked up, and for a moment their eyes held something that looked suspiciously like regret. "So, the first course is ready. We should probably get everything plated before the guests arrive."

They worked in comfortable silence after that, the easy rhythm of their partnership returning naturally. But Jamie couldn't shake the feeling that something had shifted between them, that the almost-kiss had created a new kind of tension—one that neither of them seemed quite sure how to navigate.

As they arranged the final garnish on the platter, Alex's hand brushed against Jamie's, and this time both of them jumped slightly, as if surprised by their own eagerness. They didn't pull away immediately, instead letting their fingers rest together for a moment longer than necessary.

"We make a good team," Alex said quietly.

"We do," Jamie agreed, their voice barely audible over the hum of the refrigerator.

The timer on the oven chose that moment to beep, announcing that the garlic bread was ready, and they both stepped back as if remembering where they were, what they were supposed to be doing. But the moment had been acknowledged, even if neither of them had found the courage to name it.

Later, as they carried the finished appetizers to the dining area, Jamie caught Alex looking at them with an expression that was impossible to read—part apology, part longing, and something else that Jamie couldn't quite identify. It was the same look they'd seen in their own reflection in the bathroom mirror: someone standing on the edge of something important, terrified and exhilarated by the possibility of falling.

The wine stain on the discarded shirt would probably never come out completely, Jamie realized, but maybe that was fitting. Some marks were meant to last, reminders of moments when two people had come impossibly close to finding something neither of them had expected to find in a cramped apartment kitchen.
