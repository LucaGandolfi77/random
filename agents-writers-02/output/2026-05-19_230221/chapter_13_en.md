
## Chapter 13: "The Taste Test"

The risotto sat in the wide, shallow pan like liquid silk, its surface glistening under the kitchen’s harsh fluorescent light. Jamie hovered over it, a wooden spoon in hand, watching the steam curl upward in delicate wisps. The scent of mushrooms—earthy, rich, almost fungal in its intensity—filled the cramped apartment kitchen, mingling with the lingering aroma of garlic and white wine. It was perfect, or close enough to it. They had followed Alex’s recipe to the letter, adjusting the heat as they went, their movements synchronized as if they had been cooking together for years instead of days.

Alex appeared at Jamie’s side, a dish towel slung over one shoulder, their apron smudged with flour and what looked like a suspicious green streak from the pea purée earlier. “Ready for the moment of truth?” they asked, their voice low, almost conspiratorial. Jamie nodded, but their stomach fluttered. They had tasted the risotto dozens of times during the cooking process, but this felt different—more final. A test not just of their skills, but of whatever unspoken thing had been building between them.

They ladled two portions into shallow bowls, the rice clinging together in glossy, al dente ridges. Jamie added a final flourish of grated Parmesan, watching the cheese melt slightly into the warm grains. Alex grabbed a pair of forks from the drawer, their fingers brushing Jamie’s in the process. Neither pulled away immediately.

“Here,” Alex said, holding out a forkful of risotto. “For the chef.”

Jamie’s breath hitched. They leaned forward, their lips parting slightly as the fork approached. The first bite was everything they had hoped for—creamy, with the deep umami of the mushrooms and a subtle warmth from the white wine. But it was the second bite, the one Alex fed them directly, that made their pulse stutter. Their eyes met over the rim of the bowl, and Jamie’s hand trembled just enough to smear a bit of sauce on Alex’s thumb.

“Sorry—” they started, but Alex was already lifting their hand to their mouth, licking the sauce away with deliberate slowness. The gesture was innocent, or at least it should have been. But the heat that spread through Jamie’s chest had nothing to do with the risotto.

“You’re overthinking it,” Alex said softly, their voice threaded with something like amusement. “Just taste it.”

Jamie opened their mouth obediently as Alex fed them another bite, this one smaller, more controlled. The intimacy of it—the proximity of their faces, the way Alex’s breath ghosted over their lips—made their skin prickle. They wanted to close the distance, to find out if Alex’s lips tasted as they imagined, but they forced themselves to focus on the food. The rice was flawless, each grain separate yet cohesive, the flavors balanced in a way that spoke to hours of careful attention. It was the kind of dish that made you forget you were hungry, that made you want to savor every bite.

But Jamie wasn’t sure they could savor anything with Alex watching them like that—eyes dark, lips parted, the tip of their tongue visible as they swallowed. It was unbearable and wonderful all at once.

“Your turn,” Alex murmured, and Jamie realized they had been staring. They grabbed their own fork, scooping up a bite of risotto and holding it out to Alex. The motion was awkward, their hand shaking again, but Alex took the fork without hesitation, their lips brushing Jamie’s knuckles as they ate.

“Mmm,” Alex said, their voice huskier now. “That’s… really good.”

Jamie’s laugh was nervous, high-pitched. “It’s just risotto.”

“Just risotto?” Alex arched an eyebrow, leaning closer. “You’re too modest. This is restaurant-quality stuff.”

The compliment sent an unexpected warmth through Jamie’s chest, but they shook their head. “We followed your recipe exactly. You’re the one who’s good.”

“Flattery will get you everywhere,” Alex said, but there was a softness in their tone that made Jamie’s heart race. They set their fork down with a clink, suddenly aware of how close they were standing. The counter between them felt like an afterthought, a flimsy barrier that might dissolve at any moment.

“Can I—?” Alex started, then stopped. They gestured to Jamie’s face with their free hand, and Jamie realized there was a smear of sauce on their cheek. Without thinking, Alex reached up to wipe it away, their thumb lingering just below Jamie’s lip. The touch was electric, sending sparks shooting down Jamie’s spine.

“Alex,” they whispered, their voice barely audible.

“This is… complicated,” Alex said, their gaze dropping to Jamie’s mouth. “But maybe… maybe we don’t have to figure it all out right now.”

Before Jamie could respond, the timer on the oven buzzed loudly, making them both jump. The moment shattered like glass. Alex stepped back as if burned, their hand flying to their apron to hide the telltale flush on their cheeks.

“The garlic bread,” Jamie said quickly, grateful for the distraction. They moved toward the oven, but Alex caught their arm.

“Wait.” Their voice was firmer now, edged with something like determination. “We’re doing this, aren’t we? Whatever this is.”

Jamie’s pulse quickened. “I don’t know what you mean.”

“You do.” Alex’s thumb traced a small circle on Jamie’s wrist, and they shivered. “We’ve been dancing around it since the market. Since the wine spill. Since—”

“Since the pasta dough?” Jamie finished, their voice barely a whisper.

Alex laughed, but it was tinged with relief. “Yeah. Since then.”

The honesty of it made Jamie’s knees weak. They had spent so much time pretending that their feelings were just about the food, just about the collaboration, that hearing Alex say it aloud felt both terrifying and inevitable. 

“I’m scared,” Jamie admitted, the words spilling out before they could stop them. “What if this ruins everything? What if we mess up the dinner and the friendship and—”

“We’ll cross that bridge when we come to it,” Alex interrupted, their tone gentle but firm. “Right now, we have a dinner to finish and a timer going off every five minutes.”

It was a weak joke, but it made Jamie laugh, and the tension between them eased just enough for them to focus on the task at hand. They pulled the garlic bread from the oven, its edges perfectly golden, and Jamie felt a flicker of pride. They had done this—together. Every dish, every moment of doubt, every near-miss kiss. It was all leading to this, whatever this was.

As they plated the final course, Jamie caught Alex’s eye and smiled. It was tentative, fragile, but real. And maybe that was enough for now.
