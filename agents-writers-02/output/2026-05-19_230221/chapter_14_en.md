
## Chapter 14: Friends Arrive

The first knock came just as Jamie was arranging the final garnish of microgreens on the scallop plates. The sound echoed through the cramped apartment like a drumbeat, and both cooks froze mid-motion—Jamie with tweezers poised over the delicate greens, Alex with a spoon hovering above the pea purée.

"That's them," Alex whispered, wiping their hands on a towel despite knowing they'd be back in the kitchen in seconds. "Our friends are actually here."

Jamie's heart performed an ungraceful somersault against their ribs. They set down the tweezers with deliberate care, suddenly hyper-aware of the flour dusting their forearms and the way their apron had become stained with various sauces throughout the afternoon's preparations. "How do I look?" they asked, though they barely heard their own voice over the rush of blood in their ears.

"Perfect," Alex said, but their eyes were already scanning the kitchen with the critical eye of someone who knew exactly how much work lay hidden behind the polished presentation. "Ready for this?"

The question hung between them, loaded with implications that went far beyond whether the hors d'oeuvres were properly chilled. Jamie nodded, though uncertainty twisted in their stomach like over-whipped cream. Were they ready? For the dinner party, certainly—they'd planned every detail down to the placement of serving utensils. But ready for what this night might mean for whatever was growing between them? That remained to be seen.

The second knock came with more insistence, accompanied by the muffled sound of voices and laughter filtering through the door. Alex moved first, wiping their hands again and heading toward the entryway with the fluid grace that always seemed to follow them through the kitchen.

"I've got it!" they called over their shoulder, but Jamie found themselves following anyway, drawn by the same magnetic pull that had kept them orbiting around Alex all day.

Their friends spilled into the apartment in a colorful parade of coats and greetings. Marcus arrived with his usual dramatic flair, immediately commenting on the aroma wafting from the kitchen. Sarah arrived carrying a bottle of wine and a bag of bread from the bakery down the street. Emma came last, her eyes widening as she took in the transformed space.

"Wow," she said, stepping fully into the apartment. "This might actually be the coziest dinner party I've ever attended."

Jamie felt heat rise to their cheeks, equal parts pride and anxiety. The apartment had indeed been transformed—their cramped kitchen now resembled a television cooking show set, with copper pots gleaming and fresh herbs arranged in tiny vases along the windowsill. Every surface that could hold a dish did, creating an organized chaos that spoke of hours of meticulous preparation.

"Please, make yourselves comfortable," Jamie said, though the phrase felt inadequate given the circumstances. In a space this small, comfort was relative, and every chair pulled out brought guests closer to the cooking action.

Alex reappeared from the kitchen, now wearing a clean apron and carrying two glasses of white wine. "Champagne?" they offered, but Marcus had already produced a bottle from his coat pocket.

"We brought something special," he announced, holding up the bottle with theatrical pride. "Vintage, supposedly. Though I have no idea if that's good or bad."

While the others gathered around the small dining table that had been squeezed into the living area, Jamie slipped back into the kitchen. The transformation was immediate—their shoulders relaxed, their breathing slowed to match the rhythm of the space they knew so intimately. But even here, among the familiar tools and scents, they couldn't shake the feeling of being watched, judged, found wanting.

Alex joined them moments later, leaning against the counter with a glass of wine in hand. "Nervous?" they asked quietly.

"Terrified," Jamie admitted, focusing intently on the edge of a knife blade. "What if they hate the food? What if we burn everything? What if—"

"What if we're amazing?" Alex interrupted, stepping closer. Their shoulder brushed against Jamie's arm, sending an electric current through both of them. "Because that's what's actually going to happen."

The confidence in Alex's voice was infectious, but Jamie couldn't quite share it. "You don't know that."

"Actually, I do." Alex gestured toward the dining area where their friends were settling in, laughing and chatting easily. "We've been cooking together for months. We know how this works."

But that was exactly the problem—they knew how this worked professionally. What they didn't know was how to navigate the delicate balance of romance and rivalry that had been simmering between them all day, threatening to boil over at any moment.

The first course was ready, and Jamie plated the scallops with practiced precision while Alex spooned the pea purée onto small plates. The colors were vibrant against the white porcelain—the golden-brown scallops, the bright green purée, the scattered microgreens that added a final touch of elegance.

"Beautiful," Alex murmured, and Jamie looked up to find them watching with something softer than professional admiration in their eyes.

"Let's serve," Jamie said, needing to break the spell before it became too much.

The procession from kitchen to table felt ceremonial, each step carrying the weight of their shared effort. Jamie carried the scallops while Alex handled the wine, and for a moment, they moved in perfect synchronization despite the cramped quarters. Their friends oohed and aahed appropriately, and Jamie felt some of their tension ease as genuine compliments began to flow.

"This is incredible," Sarah said, cutting into her scallop with practiced ease. "The sear is perfect, and the purée—"

"Is exactly what we practiced," Alex finished with a grin, but Jamie noticed how their voice softened when they spoke, how their eyes never left Jamie's face.

As the first course was cleared away, the kitchen became a whirlwind of activity. Jamie focused on the risotto, stirring constantly as the Arborio rice began to release its starch into the warm broth. Alex moved around them, checking temperatures and adjusting seasonings with the easy familiarity of someone who could anticipate their every need.

"How's the timing?" Alex asked, glancing at the clock mounted above the stove.

"Good, but we need to start the pasta soon," Jamie replied, tasting the risotto and adjusting the salt. "The mushrooms are ready, and I want to make sure everything comes together."

Their hands brushed as Alex reached for the parmesan, and neither bothered to pull away. Instead, they both paused for just a second, letting the contact linger longer than necessary.

"Hey, lovebirds," Marcus called from the table, "save some romance for after dinner, yeah?"

Heat flooded Jamie's face, but they were saved from having to respond by Emma's gentle laugh. "They're just excited about the food," she said, though her eyes twinkled with understanding.

The second course required more coordination, and Jamie found themselves grateful for the distraction of multiple tasks. While they finished the risotto, Alex rolled out the fresh pasta, their movements fluid and sure. The contrast between them was striking—Jamie's methodical attention to each step versus Alex's intuitive understanding of timing and texture.

"I think we make a pretty good team," Alex said, not looking up from their work.

Jamie considered this as they stirred the risotto, watching the grains of rice swell and soften in the warm liquid. "Yeah," they agreed slowly. "We do."

But even as they acknowledged it, Jamie couldn't shake the feeling that they were dancing around something important. The easy camaraderie of the kitchen felt different now—charged with possibilities that neither of them seemed quite ready to explore.

The timer dinged, and both cooks moved simultaneously toward the oven. Jamie opened the door to reveal perfectly golden garlic bread, while Alex pulled out a tray of roasted vegetables that filled the kitchen with their earthy aroma.

"Everything smells amazing," Sarah called out, and Jamie realized that their friends had fallen into a comfortable silence, watching the two cooks work with the same fascination they might afford a dance performance.

"This is better than any restaurant," Emma added, and Jamie felt a swell of pride that had nothing to do with ego and everything to do with shared accomplishment.

As they plated the main course, Jamie found themselves stealing glances at Alex. There was something mesmerizing about watching them work—the way they tasted as they cooked, the slight furrow of concentration between their brows when they were focused, the easy smile that appeared when a dish came together just right.

"You're staring," Alex said, catching their eye.

"I am?" Jamie's voice came out higher than intended.

"Definitely." Alex's smile widened. "Is it the apron? I know it's a bit stained, but—"

"No, it's not the apron." Jamie set down their serving spoon and took a step closer. "It's you. Everything about this—us—cooking together like this. It feels..."

"Perfect?" Alex supplied softly.

"Complicated," Jamie corrected, but their lips curved into a smile despite the confusion knotting their stomach.

The main course was a triumph—creamy risotto studded with wild mushrooms, perfectly al dente pasta tossed with brown butter and sage, and the roasted vegetables that added color and texture to the plate. As Jamie watched their friends savor each bite, they felt a warmth that had nothing to do with the kitchen's ambient temperature.

"This is really good," Marcus said between bites, and Jamie felt Alex's hand find theirs under the table—a brief, innocent touch that somehow communicated everything they couldn't say aloud.

But even in success, Jamie couldn't shake the underlying anxiety that had been building all evening. The pressure of hosting, of performing, of being watched while they worked—it was exhilarating and terrifying in equal measure. And layered over all of that was the knowledge that whatever happened after tonight would change everything between them.

As the plates were cleared and their friends began discussing dessert plans, Jamie found themselves momentarily overwhelmed by the chaos of the evening. The kitchen, usually a sanctuary of controlled creativity, now felt like the eye of a storm—calm in the center but surrounded by swirling activity on all sides.

"We did it," Alex said quietly, appearing at their side with two fresh glasses of wine.

Jamie took the offered glass, their fingers intertwining briefly with Alex's. "We did."

But as they looked around at the wreckage of their carefully orchestrated evening—the empty plates, the wine-stained tablecloth, the scattered crumbs that told the story of a meal well enjoyed—Jamie realized that the real test was still to come. They had successfully fed their friends, but could they navigate whatever came next between them?

The timer chimed again, announcing that the tiramisu was ready to be assembled, and both cooks moved automatically toward their stations. In the rhythm of their movements, there was something comforting—an unspoken understanding that whatever doubts plagued them individually, together they could handle whatever came next.

As they worked side by side in the fading light of evening, Jamie allowed themselves to believe that maybe, just maybe, they were ready for whatever came after the last bite was eaten and the final toast was made. The kitchen had always been where they made sense together, and tonight proved that was still true—even with an audience.
