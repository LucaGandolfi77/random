
## The Toast

The clink of wine glasses echoed through the cramped dining area, a delicate symphony that seemed to vibrate against the exposed brick walls of Jamie's apartment. Six friends had gathered around the makeshift table—a salvaged door propped on sawhorses, covered with a linen cloth that had seen better days but somehow looked perfect under the warm glow of string lights. The first course had been served with minimal disaster: seared scallops perched atop vibrant pea purée, garnished with the microgreens Alex had insisted were essential despite Jamie's initial skepticism.

Jamie stood at the head of the table, a half-empty bottle of Sauvignon Blanc in one hand and their own glass in the other. Their heart hammered against their ribs like a caged bird, and they wondered if the slight tremor in their hand was visible to everyone or just their own nervous imagination. Across the table, Alex was laughing at something Marcus had said, their head thrown back in that way that made their throat look impossibly long, their eyes crinkling at the corners like they were sharing a secret with the universe itself.

*Look at them,* Jamie thought, *completely at ease while I'm standing here trying not to hyperventilate.* They took a steadying breath, feeling the familiar weight of responsibility that came with hosting. But this wasn't just about the food anymore—it never had been.

"To new beginnings," Jamie said, their voice carrying across the intimate space. The words felt heavier than they'd intended, weighted with meaning that surely wasn't lost on Alex. They raised their glass, deliberately meeting Alex's gaze across the table. Those green eyes—so like the first shoots of spring grass—locked onto theirs with an intensity that made Jamie's breath catch.

The table fell quiet except for the distant hum of the refrigerator and the occasional pop of the radiators cooling down from the day's cooking. Sarah leaned forward, her expression softening as she watched the exchange between the two cooks.

"To new beginnings," the group echoed, their voices creating a warm harmony that wrapped around the room like steam from a perfect stock.

Jamie's eyes never left Alex's face. They saw the flush that had crept up their neck, the way their lips curved into something that wasn't quite a smile but was definitely something more than casual appreciation. There was something vulnerable in that look, something that made Jamie want to cross the room and press their forehead against Alex's, just to see if they could absorb some of that warmth into their own bones.

"Well, well," Sarah said, her voice laced with amusement as she set down her glass. "Someone's got it bad."

The comment hung in the air like the scent of thyme—subtle but unmistakable. Jamie felt their face heat up, but they couldn't look away from Alex. Their friend Emma snorted into her napkin, trying to hide her grin, while Marcus raised an eyebrow in that way he had when he was piecing together a particularly juicy story.

"You two have been eye-fucking each other all evening," Emma declared, then immediately clamped a hand over her mouth. "Oh my God, I'm sorry, I just—"

"It's fine," Alex said, their voice low and rough enough that only Jamie could hear it. They reached across the table and briefly covered Jamie's hand with their own, sending shivers up Jamie's arm that had nothing to do with the cool wine glass.

*Did they just...? No, they couldn't have. But God, I wish—*

"Seriously though," Sarah continued, undeterred by the brief interruption, "when did this happen? Because I've been sitting here watching you two like a tennis match, and I'm pretty sure the balls have been flying back and forth for weeks."

Jamie's brain short-circuited for a moment. They opened their mouth to protest, to make some joke about professional collaboration, but Alex beat them to it.

"Weeks?" Alex laughed, but there was a nervous edge to it. "Sarah, honey, I think you might be giving us too much credit. We're still figuring out if we can work together without setting off the fire alarm."

The table erupted in good-natured laughter, but Jamie noticed how Alex's knee kept bouncing under the table, betraying their calm facade. They wanted to reach out, to still that restless energy with a touch, but they were trapped by social convention and their own tangled emotions.

"Speaking of fire alarms," Marcus said, "that garlic bread was definitely charred to perfection. I've never tasted anything quite like it."

"Hey!" Alex protested, throwing a dinner roll at him. "That was intentional technique. Very hipster, very now."

"It was amazing," Jamie interjected quickly, coming to Alex's defense. "The slight bitterness added depth to the overall meal profile."

"See?" Sarah leaned back in her chair, obviously pleased with herself for having predicted this moment. "They finish each other's sentences. It's disgusting and adorable."

Jamie chanced another glance at Alex and found them watching with a small, genuine smile playing on their lips. There was something disarming about seeing Alex relaxed, unguarded in a way they rarely were during service at the restaurant. At work, they were all sharp edges and quick movements, but here, in this cramped kitchen-turned-dining-room, they seemed almost peaceful.

"Can we please stop analyzing the lovebirds and enjoy our meal?" Emma pleaded, though her eyes sparkled with mischief. "I want to hear about how you two conspired to make me gain five pounds in one sitting."

The conversation shifted naturally after that, flowing from compliments on the food to stories about their week, to plans for the upcoming weekend. But Jamie found themselves unable to focus on anything beyond Alex's presence. Every time their hands brushed while reaching for bread, every shared glance when someone told a particularly funny story, every moment of synchronized laughter—it all felt like proof that the universe was conspiring to make this evening perfect.

*Is this what it feels like?* Jamie wondered silently. *When everything aligns and suddenly you're not just existing alongside someone, but living inside the same heartbeat?*

They caught Alex's eye again and saw their own wonder reflected back at them. In that moment, surrounded by friends and the lingering aromas of their combined efforts, Jamie understood that this—this tentative, beautiful thing unfurling between them—was worth every moment of uncertainty.

"So," Marcus said, leaning back with a satisfied sigh, "when do we get to hear the official announcement? Because I'm pretty sure we've all been waiting for this since the wine spill incident."

The table went quiet again, but this time it felt different. Anticipatory. Supportive. Sarah reached over and squeezed Jamie's hand under the table, while Emma gave Alex an encouraging thumbs-up.

Jamie looked at Alex, really looked at them—the way their hair had escaped its ponytail and framed their face in messy waves, the faint smudge of flour on their cheek that they hadn't noticed, the way their lower lip caught between their teeth when they were nervous. All these small details that had somehow become the most important things in the world.

"We're still figuring it out," Jamie said softly, their voice carrying easily in the intimate space. "But... it feels like coming home, you know?"

Alex's breath hitched audibly, and they nodded slowly. "Yeah," they whispered. "Yeah, it does."

The rest of the evening passed in a blur of satisfied murmurs, empty plates, and the comfortable chaos of friends who had known each other too long to need words to communicate. Jamie and Alex moved through the motions of serving and clearing with practiced ease, their coordination seamless despite the emotional minefield they'd just navigated.

As they prepared to bring out the main course—a creamy mushroom risotto that filled the apartment with the earthy perfume of thyme and white wine—Alex lingered behind Jamie, close enough that their breath warmed the nape of Jamie's neck.

"You okay?" Alex murmured.

Jamie turned slightly, finding themselves face-to-face with those green eyes that had been haunting their dreams for weeks. "Are you?"

A slow smile spread across Alex's face, tender and hopeful. "Yeah. Yeah, I think I am."

They stood there for a moment, suspended in the golden light of the kitchen, the sounds of their friends' laughter drifting from the other room. Around them, the evidence of their labor was everywhere—in the flour dusting the countertops, in the scattered herbs, in the lingering warmth of the oven—but it was nothing compared to the heat that seemed to emanate from the space between them.

*This is it,* Jamie realized. *This is where everything changes.*

And maybe it already had.
