
## Chapter 18: The Confession

The last of their friends had finally departed, leaving behind a trail of laughter that echoed down the hallway and the lingering scent of wine and good food. Jamie stood in the doorway, watching the elevator doors close, feeling oddly deflated yet exhilarated all at once. The apartment was quiet now, except for the faint hum of the refrigerator and the occasional drip from the kitchen faucet they'd forgotten to turn off completely.

"You okay?" Alex's voice cut through the comfortable silence, laced with concern.

Jamie turned to find Alex leaning against the counter, arms crossed, flour still dusting their apron like fresh snow. Even in the dim light of the single bulb they'd forgotten to replace, Alex looked tired but happy—radiant, almost. It made Jamie's chest tighten in a way that had nothing to do with exhaustion.

"I think so," Jamie said, moving further into the apartment. "That actually went really well, didn't it? I mean, despite the burnt garlic bread and the collapsed tiramisu."

Alex laughed, that warm sound that always made Jamie's stomach flip. "Yeah, well, disasters make the best stories later. Remember my mom's lasagna? She literally set off the fire alarm."

They both chuckled, but the moment stretched, growing heavier with unspoken words. Jamie noticed how Alex wouldn't quite meet their eyes, how their fingers drummed nervously against their forearm. The same restless energy had been building between them all evening, like static electricity just waiting to discharge.

"You know," Jamie began, leaning against the counter beside Alex, "during service tonight, when I was plating the scallops, I kept looking up and seeing you over at the risotto station."

Alex's eyes flickered toward theirs then, curiosity piqued. "Yeah?"

"And I remember thinking how beautiful you looked, completely focused on what you were doing. Like you were conducting an orchestra or something." Jamie's voice dropped, becoming more intimate. "God, that sounds so cheesy."

"No, it's not cheesy," Alex replied softly. "I was watching you too. You have this way of moving around the kitchen, like you belong there more than anywhere else."

The confession hung in the air between them, fragile and significant. Jamie felt their heart racing, palms suddenly damp despite the cool evening air drifting through the open window.

"I've been thinking about you constantly," Jamie blurted, then immediately winced inwardly. *Too much, too fast.* But the words were out there now, floating in the space between them.

Alex straightened up, finally turning to face them fully. "What do you mean?"

"All week. During prep, during service, even when I'm trying to sleep. I'll be chopping vegetables and suddenly remember how you looked when you tasted that sauce, or how you laughed when we burned the bread. It's... distracting, honestly." Jamie's cheeks flushed, but they couldn't bring themselves to look away. "I know that's probably weird, considering we're supposed to be just friends and coworkers."

The vulnerability in Jamie's voice made Alex's breath catch. They'd been feeling exactly the same thing, but hearing it spoken aloud made it terrifyingly real.

"I think about you too," Alex admitted, their voice barely above a whisper. "More than I should, probably. Especially after... well, after the taste test. Do you remember? When we were feeding each other that risotto?"

Jamie nodded, remembering that moment vividly. The way Alex's lips had touched theirs, even accidentally, sending sparks through their entire body. They'd both pulled back quickly, pretending it hadn't happened, but the memory had haunted Jamie ever since.

"I couldn't stop thinking about that either," Jamie confessed. "And after the wine spill, when you were cleaning my shirt..."

"We almost kissed," Alex breathed, stepping closer until they were almost touching. "I wanted to so badly, but then I got scared."

"Scared of what?"

"Scared of ruining what we have. Scared that if we tried something and it didn't work out, I'd lose my best friend. Scared that I'm not... I don't know, that I'm not good enough for you."

Jamie reached out, gently cupping Alex's face in their hands. Their thumbs brushed against Alex's cheeks, feeling the slight stubble and warmth of skin. "Alex, look at me."

Those green eyes, usually so confident and bright, now seemed uncertain, searching. Jamie's heart ached at the vulnerability there.

"You are more than good enough," Jamie said firmly. "You're incredible. You're talented and funny and kind, and you make me feel like I can do anything when we're cooking together. I don't want to lose you either, but I also can't keep pretending that I don't want this—want you."

Alex's breath hitched. "Want me?"

"God, yes. I want you so much it's ridiculous. I want to kiss you properly, without interruptions or excuses. I want to know what it feels like when you touch me without pulling away. I want—" Jamie stopped, realizing they were rambling, but Alex was smiling softly, encouragingly.

"Tell me," Alex whispered.

"I want to wake up next to you and make coffee together. I want to argue over recipes and burn dinner and still laugh about it. I want to taste everything you cook and have you taste everything I make. I want to build something real with you, Alex. Something that lasts longer than one perfect dinner party."

Tears pricked at the corners of Alex's eyes. "Jamie, I've never... I've never felt this way about anyone before. Not like this. It's terrifying and amazing all at once."

"That's exactly how I feel," Jamie said, their own voice cracking slightly. "And I know it's scary. I'm scared too. But what if we're scared together? What if we take this chance and see where it leads?"

Alex stepped closer until their foreheads were touching, their breath mingling in the small space between them. "What if it doesn't work?"

"What if it does?"

The question seemed to settle something deep within Alex, and they nodded slowly. "Okay. Okay, let's be scared together then."

Jamie smiled, relief flooding through them. "Really?"

"Really. Because... because I think I've been falling for you since the market, when you argued with me about basil. You were so passionate, so sure of what you wanted. And I realized I wanted to know what else you felt that strongly about."

"You do know," Jamie said gently. "You've seen it in the kitchen, in how you throw yourself into every dish, how you care about every detail. That passion, that fire—it's one of the things I love most about you."

Alex's hand found Jamie's waist, pulling them closer. "I love that about you too. How you notice everything, how you care so deeply about getting things right. Even when it drives me crazy sometimes."

"Even when I'm being stubborn about technique?"

"Especially then."

Their lips were so close now that Jamie could feel Alex's warm breath against their mouth. Every nerve ending seemed to come alive, buzzing with anticipation. They'd imagined this moment so many times, but nothing compared to the reality of standing here with Alex, the person who understood them better than anyone else.

"I have to ask," Alex murmured, their voice thick with emotion. "Are you sure about this? About us?"

Jamie thought about all the moments that had led to this point—the playful arguments, the near-missed kisses, the way their hands had touched while kneading dough, the intimate taste test that had left them both flustered and wanting more. They thought about the future, about possibilities they'd never allowed themselves to consider before.

"I've never been more sure of anything," Jamie replied, and then they closed the distance between them, finally tasting the sweetness they'd both been craving.
