Based on your template, here is a 10-chapter outline for a dark fantasy novel centered around the ancient citadel.

**Working Title:** *The Sanguine Liturgy*

---

### **Chapter 1: The Carrion Toll**
*   **Setting:** A famine road leading toward the shadow of the citadel.
*   **Plot:** Elara, a survivor whose "gift" allows her to sense the decaying residue of the dead, arrives at a roadside shrine. She finds a group of starving pilgrims being terrorized by something unseen in the fog.
*   **The Personal Danger:** To save a child, Elara must use her talent, drawing the "rot" out of the air and into her own veins.
*   **The Moral Cost/Gain:** The child is saved, but Elara’s hands remain stained black for days, and the hunger she feels afterward is not for food, but for the essence of the dying.
*   **Dread:** The realization that the "blood moon" isn't just an astronomical event, but a beacon.

### **Chapter 2: The Desecrated Threshold**
*   **Setting:** The outer gates of the ancient citadel, overgrown with weeping, black flora.
*   **Plot:** Elara reaches the citadel seeking sanctuary, only to find the gates guarded by a splintered order of knights who have lost their faith. They are fighting a losing battle against "The Echoes"—shadows of the forgotten wars.
*   **The Personal Danger:** A skirmish in the narrow gatehouse where Elara must decide whether to hide or use her power to disrupt the shadows.
*   **The Moral Cost/Gain:** She disrupts the shadows, but in doing so, she accidentally "tethers" one to a dying knight, forcing him to endure an eternal, conscious dissolution.
*   **Dread:** The whispers in the halls aren't just sounds; they are memories trying to find a host.

### **Chapter 3: Sanctum of the Hollowed**
*   **Setting:** A ruined manor within the citadel walls, repurposed as a makeshift infirmary.
*   **Plot:** Elara meets Kaelen, a disgraced priest who maintains a "miracle" that keeps the plague at bay. She realizes his miracle is a lie—he is merely siphoning the sickness into himself.
*   **The Personal Danger:** A sudden outbreak of "The Red Fever" in the manor.
*   **The Moral Cost/Gain:** Elara helps Kaelen stabilize the ward, but to do so, she must tap into the citadel's ancient, corrupt wellspring, feeling the weight of every soul lost in the forgotten wars.
*   **Dread:** The sense that the citadel is breathing, reacting to their presence.

### **Chapter 4: The Weight of Whispers**
*   **Setting:** The Citadel’s Great Library, where the air is thick with dust and the smell of old parchment and copper.
*   **Plot:** Seeking a way to stop the blood moon’s influence, Elara and Kaelen search for forbidden rites. They discover that the "forgotten wars" were actually a failed attempt to cage a primordial hunger.
*   **The Personal Danger:** The library is haunted by "The Unwritten"—wraiths of scholars who died mid-thought.
*   **The Moral Cost/Gain:** They find the ritual needed to weaken the horror, but the ritual requires a "sacrifice of memory"—one of them must forget a cherished person to fuel the spell.
*   **Dread:** The realization that the horror isn't invading the citadel; it is being summoned by it.

### **Chapter 5: A Feast of Ash**
*   **Setting:** The Citadel’s banquet hall, now a site of starvation and madness.
*   **Plot:** Food supplies run out. The tension between the survivors breaks. A cult of "Moon-Eaters" emerges from the shadows, believing the carnage is a holy sacrament.
*   **The Personal Danger:** A violent confrontation in the dining hall.
*   **The Moral Cost/Gain:** Elara uses her power to paralyze the cultists, but the sensation of their collective terror feeds her, leaving her feeling physically bloated and spiritually nauseous.
*   **Dread:** The cultists aren't acting out of evil, but out of a desperate, starving hope.

### **Chapter 6: The Veins of the Earth**
*   **Setting:** The deep catacombs and cisterns beneath the citadel.
*   **Plot:** To reach the heart of the citadel, Elara and Kaelen must descend into the damp, claustrophobic tunnels where the architecture turns organic and fleshy.
*   **The Personal Danger:** The environment itself becomes predatory; walls pulse like lungs.
*   **The Moral Cost/Gain:** They bypass a collapsed tunnel by "commanding" the decay to part, but Elara’s skin begins to take on the texture of the stone, a permanent physical corruption.
*   **Dread:** The sound of footsteps behind them that match their own rhythm exactly.

### **Chapter 7: The Liturgy of Blood**
*   **Setting:** The High Temple, illuminated by the crimson light of the moon through a shattered rose window.
*   **Plot:** They reach the center, only to find the "Horror" is a manifestation of the citadel's own history—a sentient, weeping wound in reality.
*   **The Personal Danger:** Kaelen is forced to choose between his dying faith and the "corrupted miracle" required to hold the entity at bay.
*   **The Moral Cost/Gain:** Kaelen performs a miracle that saves Elara, but it requires him to become a living vessel for the plague, turning him into something neither man nor monster.
*   **Dread:** The horror begins to speak, using the voices of those Elara has failed.

### **Chapter 8: The Entanglement**
*   **Setting:** A dreamscape/liminal space created by the blood moon.
*   **Plot:** To fight the entity, Elara must enter its consciousness. She realizes that to destroy it, she cannot strike it from the outside; she must merge with it to command its hunger.
*   **The Personal Danger:** Losing her sense of self within the vast, agonizing memory of the citadel.
*   **The Moral Cost/Gain:** She gains the power to shape the horror, but she loses her ability to feel warmth or light; her soul becomes "cold" to the touch.
*   **Dread:** The terrifying intimacy of knowing the monster’s pain.

### **Chapter 9: The Breaking of the Moon**
*   **Setting:** The highest spire of the citadel, amidst a storm of ash and blood.
*   **Plot:** The final confrontation. Elara uses her corrupted power to turn the entity's hunger inward, making it consume its own history.
*   **The Personal Danger:** The physical toll of the magic begins to tear her body apart.
*   **The Moral Cost/Gain:** The ritual works, and the blood moon begins to fade, but the cost is the absolute destruction of the citadel and everyone remaining within it.
*   **Dread:** The silence that follows the violence—a hollow, empty peace.

### **Chapter 10: The Salt and the Scars**
*   **Setting:** The desolate ruins of the citadel at dawn; a grey, ash-covered wasteland.
*   **Plot:** Elara emerges from the rubble, the sole survivor. The horror is gone, but the world remains broken, famine-stricken, and plague-ridden.
*   **The Personal Danger:** The internal struggle to remain human when one’s very essence has been rewritten by darkness.
*   **The Moral Cost/Gain:** She has saved the world from a greater catastrophe, but she is now a creature of shadow, unable to ever truly rejoin humanity.
*   **Dread:** The final image of Elara walking down a famine road, her footsteps leaving black, dying patches on the earth.
