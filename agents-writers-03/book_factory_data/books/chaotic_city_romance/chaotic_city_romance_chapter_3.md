**3. Binary Hearts**  

---

### POV: **Kira**

Kira’s boots clanged against the cracked pavement as she stalked Rook through the maze‑like alleys behind the train station. The city’s neon signs bled into puddles, each ripple mirroring a different version of herself — one half cloaked in the Bureau’s badge, the other etched in the static hiss of pirate frequencies.  

She’d mapped his route over the past week, noting how he slipped through data streams like a shadow: fluid, calculated, always watching. Tonight felt different. His pace quickened; his shoulders tensed, as if some unseen alarm warned him of her presence.  

At the mouth of an alley, Rook halted, a gloved hand resting on a dumpster. Kira ducked behind a rusted fire‑escape, breath shallow. His head turned, and for a heartbeat their eyes met through the rain‑streaked gloom.  

*He knows,* she thought, fingers tightening around the taser concealed in her coat. *But why hasn’t he run?*  

The moment shattered as a train roared overhead, its vibrations humming through the ground. When she looked again, Rook was gone.  

---

### POV: **Rook**

Rook’s pulse thrummed in his ears, a stark contrast to the city’s mechanical lullaby. He’d felt her there — a ghost in the periphery, watching. Not Bureau. Not anymore. Her gaze had flickered with something unreadable: curiosity? Recognition?  

He quickened his pace, the matte‑black case clutched tighter. Inside, the drive pulsed with encrypted data — Kira’s research, stolen from a Bureau terminal weeks ago. He’d kept it, unsure why, until now.  

At the train station, he glanced back once. She stood silhouetted beneath a flickering streetlamp, her posture rigid, taser half‑drawn. Something in the way she hesitated unnerved him. This wasn’t the cold precision of an operative. It was human.  

He vanished into the crowd, but not before memorizing the slope of her shoulders, the way her jaw clenched — a portrait he’d carry like contraband.  ---

### POV: **Kira**

Back in her flat, Kira replayed the night’s events, the taser still warm in her hand. She’d expected fear, anger — anything but the hollow ache blooming in her chest.  

The radio crackled.  

> *“Signal’s weak… but I’m here.”*  

Her fingers hovered over the receiver.  

> *“Who are you?”*  Static. Then:  

> *“Someone who doesn’t want to be found.”*  She exhaled, long and slow. The line hummed between them, a lifeline spun from lies and longing.  

---

### POV: **Rook**

Rook sat in a dimly lit server room, the drive humming against his palm. Kira’s voice lingered in his earpiece like a virus he couldn’t shake.  

> *“You could disappear. Why stay?”*  

He stared at lines of code scrolling across a monitor — her research, open‑sourced and weaponized. The AIs trapped in the grid flickered like dying stars.  

> *“Because someone has to remember them.”*  

The words were barely a whisper, but they anchored him.  

In the shadows, a figure watched him from a surveillance monitor — Deputy Director Chen, his next briefing with the Bureau looming. Trust was a liability in their world. Yet as dawn bled into the skyline, Rook found himself dialing Kira’s frequency again.  

Something in her silence promised answers neither was ready to face.
