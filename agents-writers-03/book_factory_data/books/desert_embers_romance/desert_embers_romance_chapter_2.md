**Chapter 2 – “The Weight of the Maps”**  

The field beneath them was a graveyard of topographical charts, their ink smeared by rain and dust. Mara’s breath hitched as Eli’s hand clutched hers—a shaky anchor in the crumbling expanse. Behind them, the timekeeper’s radio crackled, broadcasting a map gridlocked with static, doubling the stakes. She remembered her childhood: her sister’s laughter, the way she would fold maps into pockets, the ache of losing them all. Eli’s eyes tightened; whenever they had shared a sector, his mask twitched, exposing the tremor in her grip.  

A flicker of movement caught their eye, a shadow darting past—a distant explosion or a Gestapo patrol? The battlefield’s silence surged, thick as ash. Mara wondered whether love could survive the war’s teeth—or if it only bit deeper. Eli’s grip tightened, not from urgency, but from something raw—a promise buried beneath ranks and duty. *We’re both prisoners*, she thought, *and prisoners of each other’s ambitions*.  

A misread signal struck: Mara interpreted Eli’s retreat as withdrawal from her—not toward the sanctuary of her love. The radio crackled—a false alarm nearby or a trap. They exchanged a glance, their voices overlapping barely: *Nothing changes*. The marks etched around their eyes—too sharp, too pronounced—were a treaty’s artery threatening rupture. Their hands brushed again, deliberate and simultaneous—a fleeting contact across the inked concessions they both feared.  

Just then, the medic let out a brittle, wry laugh, his coffee‑smeared jaw pressing against Eli’s palm. “Classic.” Yet a fragile warmth surged, like the tip of a fuse—*look at me*. A lie, perhaps, but enough for now. Above, lightning cleaved the sky; they froze. It was not the safer truth nor the treacherous one. It was the raw, bleeding present—a choice whispered in every step, every word, every tremor beneath her palm. And the choice, she realized, could either carve them apart or bind them together in undoing.
