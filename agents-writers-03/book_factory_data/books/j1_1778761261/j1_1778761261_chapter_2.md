### **Overall Comments**

1. **Tone & Voice** – The original maintains a crisp, slightly distanced third‑person voice that mirrors Elara’s meticulous interior life. The edits preserve that tone while tightening the prose, allowing the reader to stay inside the characters without being distracted by unnecessary clauses.

2. **Show, Don’t Tell** – In several places I replaced “she said” or “she thought” with more immediate actions (e.g., *“she replied”* or *“her thumb moved”*). This makes the moments feel lived rather than narrated.

3. **Economy of Metaphor** – Some metaphors were repeated (e.g., “the thing… like a dog worrying a bone”) and occasionally ran a beat long. I kept the most vivid image and trimmed the rest, ensuring each metaphor lands with impact.

4. **Consistency of Detail** – Small details—such as the color‑coded planner, the cortado, the “weight forward” stance—are crucial for character definition. I kept them but placed them where they had the greatest emotional resonance, avoiding redundancy.

5. **Pacing** – The chapter’s rhythm is deliberately measured, mirroring the “in‑between” space both characters inhabit. By tightening sentence length in moments of high tension (the conversation at the table, the departure) and expanding slightly during reflective beats (the description of the café, the memory of childhood), I kept the pacing aligned with the emotional stakes.

6. **Grammar & Punctuation** – A number of run‑on sentences have been split, commas repositioned, and parallel structures aligned to improve readability without sacrificing the lyrical quality of the prose.
