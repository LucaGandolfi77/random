## 2. Dialogue Polishing

Below is a **cleaned‑up version of the exchange** that tightens the pacing while preserving its emotional weight:

```text
“Something I just saw,” he murmured, eyes never leaving the grainy map on his tablet. “It looks… almost like it was drawn on the earth.”

Elara lifted the notebook, the corners already softened by use. “You mean… maybe I’m older?”

He smiled, a flicker of warmth. “Maybe. But perhaps I’m only late because I’m still learning how to read the signs.”

Silence settled, as thick as the rain that began to spit on the cobblestones outside. Then he leaned closer, his voice barely louder than the distant hum of traffic.

“If I see more of this, maybe the world has to shift.”  
Elara’s throat tightened. “Or maybe I’ve just been waiting longer than I should.”  

She hesitated, then let the words spill:  
“Whatever comes next… I want us to be ready for it—together.”
```

**Why this works:**

* **Economy of words** – each line contributes to either character insight or forward momentum.  
* **Subtle subtext** – “late because I’m still learning how to read the signs” hints at his own vulnerability without overt exposition.  
* **Parallel structure** – the two characters echo each other’s doubts (“older"/“late”), reinforcing the shared stakes.

A lavender haze draped the city beyond the billboard‑crowned spires. Elara perched in the booth, listening to the city’s murmurs—digital whispers that formed a faint rhythm only she seemed to notice. She lifted her hand, hovering just above the table’s cool metal edge. The city outside blurred into velvet soot, but inside, a new clarity settled.

> “Whatever comes next… I want us to be ready for it—together.”
