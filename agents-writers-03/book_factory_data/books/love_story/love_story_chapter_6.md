### Chapter 1 – The Friction of Proximity  

The train shuddered to a stop, a metallic groan that seemed to swallow the whole city whole. Clara’s legs caught the clatter of her duffel, its fabric brushing the floor like a wave she hadn’t meant to ride. She clutched the handletoggle, fingers trembling—not from cold, but from the memory lodged there: a tectonic pressure she couldn’t name.  

She noticed Elias first by the way his shoulders angled, scanning the crowd like a cataloguer sorting through misplaced files. His hands, calloused and precise, rested on the planner in front of him as if it might dislodge him from the shell he built around himself. She moved toward him—not because she needed his help, but because he needed her awareness; her gaze on him was a silent question posed between breaths.  

*He looked like he was counting something*, she thought, a spark thrumming beneath her irritation. *Just numbers. Just risks.* But when her thumb brushed the worn edge of the notebook, something shifted. Not a solution, not a decision—just a nod, a breath, and the sudden weight of something soon‑to‑be‑unsaid settling between them.  

Her eyes flicked to the mural behind them—the band playing on an internal streaming service—its colors fading into a wash as the flickering light captured the same shadow of dust on the wall beside her. That was the secret formula, he knew. The quiet geometry. The sign he’d once overlooked now glowing in the tear‑ducts of her memory.  

“Sorry,” she whispered, the word sliding out not as apology but as survival. She pressed her lips close, her nails tracing the ridge where a graffiti smear had marked itself. “Is that…?”  His fingers tightened on the planner, not holding her tighter, but closing a scene he hadn’t noticed at all. Somewhere in the back of his mind a clock ticked against them—the kind that measures not days, but the invisible distances they’d built between—*before* he let it in.  

Clara turned to retreat; the silence stung sharper than her own breath. Outside, the city thrummed with its own pulse, oblivious to the biology spiraling inside.
