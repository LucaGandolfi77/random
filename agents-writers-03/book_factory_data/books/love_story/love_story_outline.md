This outline follows your template, focusing on the "friction" of two people trying to be soft in a world that rewards hardness, cynicism, and efficiency.

**Title Idea:** *The Geometry of Touch*
**Protagonists:** 
*   **Elias:** A high-level architectural restorer. He values structural integrity and permanence because his own upbringing was transient. He views emotional intimacy as a "structural flaw."
*   **Clara:** A digital strategist for a fast-paced tech firm. She lives in a world of metrics, swipes, and ephemeral connections. She uses cynicism as armor to avoid the disappointment of "temporary" people.

---

### Chapter 1: The Friction of Proximity
*   **Setting:** A crowded, sterile commuter train during rush hour.
*   **The Beat:** Elias and Clara are forced into each other's personal space. A small moment—Elias steadying Clara as the train jolts—creates a brief, electric physical awareness that both immediately try to rationalize away.
*   **Intimacy/Obstacle:** Introduction of their "armor." They recognize the spark but both internally categorize the other as "a distraction."
*   **Emotional Risk:** A shared look that lasts a second too long, breaking the unspoken rule of urban anonymity.

### Chapter 2: The Shared Project
*   **Setting:** A high-end, glass-walled corporate office.
*   **The Beat:** They are assigned to a collaborative project (restoring a historic building for a tech conglomerate). They must work closely.
*   **Intimacy/Obstacle:** Intellectual attraction. They realize they speak the same language of detail, but their methods clash (his precision vs. her efficiency).
*   **Misreading:** Clara interprets Elias’s silence as judgment; Elias is actually just observing how her hands move when she’s frustrated.

### Chapter 3: The Cracks in the Surface
*   **Setting:** A late-night cafe near the construction site.
*   **The Beat:** A heavy rainstorm forces them to wait out the weather together. The conversation shifts from work to "why we do what we do."
*   **Intimacy/Obstacle:** They share a piece of their past—small, unvarnished truths. The "world" feels distant here, but the fear of being *seen* keeps them from leaning in.
*   **Revelation:** They realize they both use their careers to avoid being "unsettled."

### Chapter 4: The Social Pressure
*   **Setting:** A loud, performative industry gala.
*   **The Beat:** They attend as "colleagues," but the environment is hyper-competitive and transactional. They see how everyone else interacts: shallow, fast, and guarded.
*   **Intimacy/Obstacle:** They find themselves in a corner, creating a "micro-world" just for them.
*   **Emotional Risk:** Elias defends Clara against a condescending colleague. It’s a small gesture of loyalty that feels dangerously like "taking a side."

### Chapter 5: The False Start
*   **Setting:** Elias’s quiet, organized apartment.
*   **The Beat:** A moment of high tension leads to a near-kiss or a highly charged physical closeness. 
*   **Intimacy/Obstacle:** The "Timing" conflict. Clara receives a career-defining opportunity that requires her to move/travel, highlighting the impossibility of "closeness" in their current lives.
*   **Misreading:** Clara pulls away, thinking Elias is only interested in the physical, while Elias pulls away because he’s terrified of the emotional weight.

### Chapter 6: The Silence of Distance
*   **Setting:** Various—split between their separate, lonely daily routines.
*   **The Beat:** A period of professional-only communication. The "world" feels heavier now; the lack of their connection makes their lives feel more mechanical.
*   **Intimacy/Obstacle:** The internal struggle. They are both realizing that "safety" (being alone) feels a lot like emptiness.
*   **Behavior Change:** Elias stops obsessing over perfection in his work; Clara starts slowing down her digital life to actually *feel* her surroundings.

### Chapter 7: The Breaking Point
*   **Setting:** The historic site they are restoring, mid-renovation.
*   **The Beat:** A practical crisis occurs at the site (a structural error or a setback). The stress strips away their professional masks.
*   **Intimacy/Obstacle:** A heated argument that is actually about their inability to be vulnerable. 
*   **Revelation:** The argument boils over into a confession of why they are afraid: the fear that being "known" is the same as being "controlled."

### Chapter 8: The Vulnerability Test
*   **Setting:** A quiet, domestic space (perhaps a walk through a park or a simple meal).
*   **The Beat:** After the explosion, there is a tentative attempt at peace. No grand gestures, just "showing up."
*   **Intimacy/Obstacle:** They attempt to bridge the gap without the distraction of work or the noise of the world.
*   **Emotional Risk:** Elias admits he doesn't want to be "structured" if it means being alone. Clara admits she is tired of being "efficient" if it means being empty.

### Chapter 9: The Choice
*   **Setting:** A family gathering or a high-stakes professional crossroads.
*   **The Beat:** Clara is presented with the "safe" choice (the move/the job) and Elias is faced with the "stable" choice (staying in his shell).
*   **Intimacy/Obstacle:** The friction between "what I should do for my life" and "what I want for my heart."
*   **Behavior Change:** Instead of reacting out of fear, they communicate their needs clearly, even if the outcome is uncertain.

### Chapter 10: The Geometry of Us
*   **Setting:** An emotionally charged, quiet space—perhaps the finished building they worked on.
*   **The Beat:** They meet not because they *have* to, but because they *choose* to. The world is still fast, cold, and distracted, but they have built a sanctuary in each other.
*   **Intimacy/Obstacle:** They acknowledge that there are no guarantees, only the intention to remain present.
*   **Satisfying Payoff:** A moment of profound, embodied intimacy—not necessarily a wedding or a move, but a shared, quiet realization that they are no longer alone in the crowd.
