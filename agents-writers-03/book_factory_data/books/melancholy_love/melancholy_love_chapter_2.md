## 3. Structural & Stylistic Tweaks  

1. **Tighten the “catalogues them” sentence**  
   - **Original:** “He catalogues them—*risk index, commitment level, emotional bandwidth*—and stores the data in the back of his mind.”  
   - **Edit:** “He files those moments under **risk index, commitment level, emotional bandwidth**—a mental spreadsheet he stores on autopilot.”  
   *Why?* It removes the en‑dash dash and makes the internal “spreadsheet” metaphor clearer.

2. **Replace the “risk‑index, commitment level, emotional bandwidth” inventory** with a single concise phrase.  
   - **Suggestion:** “He files those details into his **risk index**, **commitment level**, and **emotional bandwidth**—a three‑column ledger he runs on autopilot.”  
   *Why?* It preserves the three categories while tightening the wording.

3. **Clarify the “beat” markers**  
   - *Option A:* Keep the asterisks but italicise a thought instead of a beat label.  
   - *Example:* “*A flash of misreading.*” → “*He assumes burnout—until he sees the tremor in her hand.*”  
   - *Why?* It converts a production‑script notation into a natural narrative pause.

4. **Trim the “protective instinct” exposition**  
   - **Original:** “He feels a faint protective instinct, the kind he reserves for junior analysts who might crumble under pressure.”  
   - **Edit:** “A faint protective instinct rises—reserved for junior analysts who might crumble.”  
   *Why?* It removes extra words while preserving the meaning.

5. **Show, don’t tell, the “risk exposure” notion**  
   - **Original:** “registers as *risk exposure*: a breach in his controlled environment.”  
   - **Edit:** “registers as **risk exposure**, a breach in his otherwise flawless ledger.”     *Why?* The metaphor of a ledger makes the abstract notion concrete.

6. **Make the “emotional bandwidth” spike more visceral**  
   - **Original:** “The data point *emotional bandwidth* spikes…”  
   - **Edit:** “The **emotional bandwidth** metric spikes—an unquantifiable weight settling in his chest.”  
   *Why?* It foregrounds the bodily sensation rather than the abstract data point.

7. **Replace the checklist at the end with an internal monologue**  
   - **Current ending:** “*Structural Integrity Checklist (internal to Elias & Clara):*” list of bullet points.  
   - **Suggested rewrite:** “Elias catalogues three data points: the misread burnout, the brief contact, the lingering question of risk. He files them away, but the last one refuses to be filed.”  
   *Why?* It keeps the analytical tone inside his head rather than presenting an external cheat sheet.
