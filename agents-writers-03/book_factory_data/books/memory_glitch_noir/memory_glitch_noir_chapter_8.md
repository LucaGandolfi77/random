**Chapter 8 – The VoidBetween Frames (Edited)**  

The first thing I noticed was the smell—ozone and burnt hair, like a server farm after a fire. They’d brought me to an old black‑site in Aethelgard, a relic from the pre‑Glass‑Spire era. The door hissed shut behind me, sealing with a pneumatic sigh that echoed off concrete walls.  A woman in a moth‑eaten trench coat stepped from the shadows. Her eyes were milky, clouded over with cataracts—or worse—so I couldn’t tell in the dim red emergency lighting.  

“You’re the one they call the stitcher,” she rasped. “We’ve been waiting.”  

The Identity Purists. Fanatics who’d swapped augments for rusted revolvers and a grudge against the mesh. I’d heard whisper‑rumors of them in the data markets—ghost stories to scare new recruits. I never imagined they’d be real.  

They strapped me into a chair at the center of the room, cables snaking from the armrests to a jury‑rigged neural‑link array that looked like it had been cobbled together from junkyard scraps. The lead Purist, a scarred man named Cobb, leaned over me with a syringe full of shimmering silver fluid.  

“This’ll open your port,” he said. “Then we’ll see what Aethelgard planted in that pretty head of yours.”  

I laughed, a hoarse, blood‑stained sound. “You think I’m the one with the bugs? Honey, you’re about to learn what happens when you pull on a thread in a sweater woven from lies.”  

The needle jabbed into my neck, and the world dissolved into static.  

---  

The interrogation wasn’t pain; it was *dismantling*. Every memory, every fragment of data I’d ever stitched became a weapon turned inward. They were hunting for the Seed File’s key—the so‑called “dead‑man’s switch.” Instead, they found the Meadow.  

It bloomed in my mind like a virus given form: emerald grass, a sky so blue it hurt, the phantom pressure of rain on skin I’d never felt. I’d locked that garden behind layers of code, a secret refuge in a world of steel and neon. Now it surged forward, raw and unfiltered.  

The Purists’ tech sparked and died. Cobb staggered back, clutching his head as sensory overload crashed through his implants. Colors bled into sounds; the scent of petrichor shorted his optics.  “What—what did you do?” he rasped.  

I grinned, blood trickling from my nose. “I let you see the sky.”  

But the Meadow wasn’t just memory—it was a weapon, as I’d learned from the tech‑priest’s cryptic warnings. Every weapon has a cost.  

The feedback loop hit like a sledgehammer. My vision whited out, then fractured into a thousand glitching shards. My neural pathways rewired themselves, burning out synapses that had taken decades to forge. And then I saw it—clear, cold, in the code itself—a hidden layer, a backdoor branded with Aethelgard’s sigil.  

My talent wasn’t mine. It was theirs. A sleeper key, planted to monitor their experiments. Every memory I’d ever touched, every client I’d ever helped, had been logged, catalogued, and filed away.  

The irony wasn’t lost on me. I’d spent years thinking I was the ghost in the machine. Turns out, I was the machine all along.  

---  

I woke up in a dumpster three blocks from the black site, my skull throbbing with the ghost‑hunger of missing memories. The Meadow was gone—burned out of my neural core like a faulty circuit. All that remained was a hollow ache, a yawning chasm where years of my life should’ve been.  

The Purists fled, their operation compromised, but Cobb’s last words echoed in my skull:  

“They’re gonna come for you now. Aethelgard doesn’t forgive loose ends.”  

Loose end. That’s what I’ve always been.  

I staggered to my feet, my body screaming in protest, and checked my port. The physical damage was repairable—a dented casing, a fried connector. The real wound was deeper. Without the Meadow, I couldn’t remember the feel of rain, the sound of my sister’s laugh, or the color of her eyes before they’d been edited into static.  

All I had left was the truth—and a target on my back the size of a corporate empire.  

The city’s neon haze blurred around me as I melted into the crowd, another ghost hunting the edges of a story that was already writing its ending without me.
