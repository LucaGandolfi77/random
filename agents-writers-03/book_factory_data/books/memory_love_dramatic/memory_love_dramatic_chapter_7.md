## 2. Line‑by‑Line Edits

### Opening / Atmosphere

> **Original**  
> The hearing room smelled of recycled air and lemon polish—something sanitized, something that couldn’t cover the way the room hummed beneath its own silence. Three hundred seats arranged in a curved amphitheater, the upper tiers dark, the lower tier a furnace of camera light. Every seat occupied. Every lens a judgment.

**Edited**  The room tasted of recycled air and lemon polish—clean, clinical, a veneer that couldn’t mask the low thrum that vibrated through the floorboards. Three hundred chairs formed a shallow amphitheater; the upper tiers were swallowed in darkness, the lower tier bathed in a furnace of camera light. Every seat was taken. Every lens watched, cold and unblinking.

*Why*: “Tasted” and “bathed” echo the sensory language while tightening the rhythm. “Cold and unblinking” makes the lenses feel like an active judgment.

### Evelyn’s Physical Anchor

> **Original**  
> Evelyn Thorne stood at the podium and pressed her palms flat against its surface until the wood bit back.

**Edited**  
Evelyn pressed the flat of her palms into the podium until the wood seemed to bite at her skin, a reminder that she was still flesh in a world built of signal.

*Why*: “Seemed to bite” keeps the metaphor alive while hinting at her physical sensation.

### The Scar Metaphor (Move it earlier)

> **Original**  
> *Don't blink. Don't breathe too loud. Don't let them see the scar.*  
> Her mother's scar—the one on her wrist, raised and pale—wasn't visible now. The one that mattered was deeper.

**Edited**  
*“Don’t blink. Don’t breathe too loud. Don’t let them see the scar on your wrist.”*  
Her mother’s warning had always lived in the raised, pale ridge on Evelyn’s skin—though now it was hidden beneath the sleeves of her coat, the real scar ran deeper: a memory that could be sold, erased, taxed.

*Why*: We hear the instruction *and* the scar in one beat, tying the two together.

### Senator Pryce’s Entry

> **Original**  
> The senator's name was Pryce. He had the face of a man who'd never been surprised by anything and found this to be a virtue. His committee was seven, but only four spoke. The others watched like sentinels, their silence a kind of language.

**Edited**  
Senator Julian Pryce entered the chamber with the expression of a man whose only surprise was that anything surprised him—a stoic virtue he wielded like a shield. Seven men and women sat on his committee; four spoke, the rest observed like silent sentinels, their stillness a language of power.

*Why*: “Stoic virtue” replaces a vague “found this to be a virtue.” “Shield” gives a concrete image.

### Evelyn’s Opening Line (Public Speaking Pressure)

> **Original**  
> *Consumer autonomy. Corporate integrity. Baseline extraction.*  
> But the letter was in the neural sheath in her coat pocket, and the letter said something different.

**Edited**  
*Consumer autonomy. Corporate integrity. Baseline extraction.*  
She’d rehearsed those phrases until they rang like a mantra. The neural sheath in her coat pocket, however, held a different script—one that began with a different question.

*Why*: “Mantra” hints at the rehearsed quality; “different script” keeps the mystery.

### Senator’s Question About Freedom

> **Original**  
> “Consent, in a system where opting out means financial ruin, is a word we’ve borrowed from a world that no longer exists.”

**Edited**  
“Consent, in a world where opting out means financial ruin, is a word we’ve borrowed from a past that no longer exists.”

*Why*: Shortens the clause while sharpening the contrast.

### Miri’s Observation (Make It More Physical)

> **Original**  
> The way Evelyn's throat moved when she swallowed—a small, involuntary contraction, the tendons in her neck pulling taut as if someone had placed a hand on her collarbone and pressed. The way her left hand found the edge of the podium and stayed there, fingertips whitening, a grip that said *I am still here, I am still deciding what to do with my body in this room.*

**Edited**  
The contraction in Evelyn’s throat was a tiny knot of muscle as she swallowed, tendons tightening as if a hand pressed against her collarbone. Her left hand found the podium’s edge and lingered, fingertips bleaching white—a silent plea that she was still *here*, still in control of a body that wanted to retreat.

*Why*: “Silent plea” adds an emotional layer; “bleaching white” makes the visual punchier.

### The “Voluntary” Question

> **Original**  
> “And what about the voluntary transactions? The ones people consent to freely?”

> *“Freely.” The word hung in the air like ozone.

**Edited**  
“What about the voluntary transactions—*the ones people consent to freely*?”  
*Freely.* The word hung in the air like a spark of ozone.

*Why*: “Spark of ozone” gives a more vivid, jarring image.

### The Letter Fragment Reveal

> **Original**  
> The dossier contained copies of the letter.  > The room went quiet. Not the performative quiet of hearings—the real kind, the kind that has weight.

**Edited**  
The dossier arrived as a thick, sealed packet, bound in ThorneTech’s sleek watermark—*not* forged by the opposition, but by someone inside. Inside lay a single sheet of paper: three stark lines lifted from Elena Voss’s 1994 correspondence.

*The room went quiet. Not the rehearsed hush of oversight, but a palpable weight, as if the very air had settled on their throats.*

*Why*: “Lifted” clarifies the source; “palpable weight” reinforces the atmosphere.

### Evelyn’s Collapse (Finish the Fragment)

> **Original**  
> *Say something. Finish the sentence. Tell them what the letter actually says…*

> **Edited**  
She wanted to say: *“No one would feel the loss, because the loss would be unremembered.”*  
Instead she choked on the half‑sentence, and the word *loss* cracked and spilled into the chamber before it could be given shape.

*Why*: Provides the exact line she wants to utter, then shows the failure more starkly.

### Evelyn’s Public Exit

> **Original**  
> “The Memory Tax proposal is under review. ThorneTech will cooperate fully with any inquiry.”

> She stepped back… applause came—not for her, not for the statement, but for the room itself…

**Edited**  
“The Memory Tax proposal is under review,” she said, her voice flat as a dead channel. “ThorneTech will cooperate fully with any inquiry.”  
She stepped back from the podium. Applause swelled—not for her or for the words she’d just uttered, but for the empty ritual itself, the performance that demanded applause even after the curtain had fallen.

*Why*: “Flat as a dead channel” mirrors the world’s media‑saturated tone; “empty ritual” emphasises the hollow victory.

### Transition to Miri’s Exit

> **Original**  
> Outside, the city was doing what it always did—converting everything into signal.

**Edited**  Outside, the city continued its endless conversion of everything into signal. Neon billboards cycled ThorneTech’s slogans: *Remember More. Forget Less. Your Memories, Optimized.* Rain slicked the pavement, reflecting the neon from the market district like a rumor spilling across borders.

*Why*: Keeps the world‑building crisp.

### Ren’s Alert (Clarify UI)

> **Original**  
> Ren.  
> *You saw it.*

**Edited**  
Her implant buzzed—a sub‑cutaneous tremor Ren had coded as “Alert #1.”  
*You saw it?* the message read.

*Why*: Naming the frequency (“Alert #1”) clarifies that it’s technical, not just “buzzing.”

### Miri’s Decision (Strengthen Agency)

> **Original**  
> *Now I go home. And I read the letter again. And I figure out what she was trying not to say.*

**Edited**  
She turned away from the crowd, slipped into the alley behind the vending machine, and let the night swallow her. Tomorrow she would go home, reread the letter, and finally hear what Evelyn was trying *not* to say.

*Why*: “Swallow her” gives a stronger visual of escape.
