## 19. Structural & Thematic Tweaks  

1. **Clarify the “Ledger” Early**  
   - In the opening chapter or an earlier scene, give the reader a quick hint (a line of dialogue, a visual flash) that the Ledger is a *record of erased souls* used by Arctos to bind consciousnesses. This eliminates the need for an expository dump later.  

2. **Make Lily’s Agency Explicit**  
   - Right now Lily appears as a flickering hologram and later as a “template.” To heighten emotional stakes, insert a brief flashback or memory where Lily *actively* resists Arctos (e.g., “She had tried to shut the Engine down once, and the scar on her wrist was a reminder of that night”). This makes her more than a plot device.  

3. **Consistent Use of Italic vs. Bold vs. CAPS**  
   - You’re using *italic* for internal thoughts, **bold** for UI text, and **CAPS** for shouted dialogue. Keep that convention uniform throughout. For example, when a data‑chip “inscribes” itself, use **bold** or ALL CAPS but not both, e.g., **YOU CHOSE THIS. NOW YOU WILL PAY.**  

4. **Pacing of the Action Beats**  
   - The fight scene currently runs on a single breathless paragraph. Break it into three distinct beats:  
     a) *The blast and the chip grab* → short, punchy sentences.  
     b) *Kael’s ultimatum* → a dialogue beat with a pause.  
     c) *Nyssa’s revelation* → a longer internal monologue.  
   - This gives the reader room to absorb each high‑stakes revelation.  

5. **Tighten Technical Jargon**  
   - “Resonances,” “Engine’s using your implant to anchor itself,” and “pulse grenade” are cool, but they can be grounded with a tactile metaphor:  
     - “The implant’s resonance was being rewired—like rewiring a speaker to a different frequency.”  
     - “The pulse grenade emitted a burst that scrambled the drones’ targeting nets, turning their own sensors against them.”
