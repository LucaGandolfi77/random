#### Chapter 6 NEON GHOST  

The hotel lobby’s sputtering neon hissed like a predator stalking its prey. I stood inside the Engine—no longer a separate machine but a bleeding lattice of glitching code fused to my synapses. Copper and cerulean rods strobbed across the room, bouncing off the stranger now facing me. She smelled of ozone and freshly‑sharpened steel.  

“Ghost,” she said, nodding at the chrome‑plated mess of her hair. A smirk cracked across her mouth. “Bad name for a girl. Better than a dead man’s.”  I glared. My implant screamed. The lullaby—a weighted thread of Lily’s voice—wrapped around her, pulling her toward the thing I’d become. She lunged, knife flashing; I moved as if the data streams in my skull bled straight through me. She skidded backward, weapon clattering.  

“You’re not *him*,” she hissed, breath ragged. “Same marks. Same stupid, fucking lullaby.” Her knife caught a wall‑sconce beam, etching a spark onto her sleeve. “Work with me, courier. They’re scanning the Marrow.”  

“Who?”  

“Kor Ravn. Arctos loyalist. He wants to scrape the Engine from your skull and sell it back to Thorne’s new masters— that’s why you’re here.”  

I had no memory of agreeing, but my body hummed with recognition.  

“Kor’s mad,” she continued. “Thinks he can monetize your half‑brain. Says you’re the key to rebooting the whole system.”  

“And you?” I demanded. “What do *you* want?”  

“Access.” She spat the word. “You’re a ghost version of the man who poached my neural data ten years ago. I’ll trade you a shiv for the ledger.”  “The ledger?”  

“Your sister’s contacts. The real origin of the Kor Moon. Why they resurrected you.”  

Silence settled, the Engine roaring in my ears.

### **— Two Minutes Later —**  

Rain flooded the alleyway, liquid chrome pooling at our boots. Surveillance drones pulsed red above us, each scan a blade turned toward our heads. She led me down a cracked service ladder, boots splashing past hitchhiker rats and fried holo‑bills.  

“This way,” she growled, kicking the door open. Inside, maintenance drones gulped oil like they were drinking. We reached a storage vault; the locks hissed.  “Hack the mainframe,” I muttered. The Engine obeyed, its voice a dissonant vibration in the walls. Locks dissolved. A terminal flickered alive.  

“Corporate killroom,” she spat. “Something’s amplifying the Engine—a feed into Kor’s rig.”  

A klaxon blared; drones descended, weapons hot. She vaulted onto a crate, shouting a string of curses. I lunged for a ruptured coolant pipe, the Engine’s scream warping the metal into a jagged blade.  

---  

### **— Three Minutes Later —**  

We were both bleeding, her neck nearly slit, her body riddled with laser cuts. The drones retreated, their systems fried by my screams.  

“You’re lying,” I said. “The ledger?”  

She jabbed a data‑spike into the vault’s terminal. Files bled across the screen: a registry of cloned consciousnesses, each **stamped** with my sister’s neural signature.  

“Someone cloned your sister,” I breathed. “Including the ledger.”  

She froze. “Not a clone. A *map*. Kor’s using your… your *ghost* to reconstruct the Kor Moon’s neural lattice. If he figures out the Engine’s protocol, it’s a backdoor to every Arctos vault.”  

“Then why help?”  

“Because you’re both of us, courier. The dead sister, the living mistake. I’m not letting you become *her*.” Her voice cracked. “I need to erase what they turned me into too.”

### **— Six Minutes Later —**  

The city’s scaffolding groaned, and reality itself bled pixels. The rival courier—Ravn, I now knew—stood atop a rusted train car, her blade in hand.  

“Yield,” she called. “Or die screaming.”  

I turned, the Engine’s logic surging. “Proposition.”  

“What?”  

“Allow me to reboot your cortex. Merge your ledger with mine. Together, we erase the boys who wronged us.”  

She hesitated. “Thorne’s client is an Arctos killer. The ones who cloned Lily? They’re selling her mind as propaganda.”  

I smiled. The Engine’s scream tore through the alley, a war anthem.  

“Allies?” I offered.  

“Enemies,” she spat—and lunged.
