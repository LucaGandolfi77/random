The rain hammered the cracked skylights of Sector 7, each drop a drumbeat that turned the silence into a dull thrum. My ocular implant flared as the lullaby surged again, static‑laced and startlingly like Lily’s laugh. I could taste copper on my tongue—old blood, fresh fear.

Heads, shoulders, briefcases flickered like fireflies—every hand that ever steadied a drink on the Korean Sea, every bone that ever sank beneath its tide. A distant alarm shrilled: **ALERT – 3RD WINDOW.** The signal pressed against the walls like cold fingers. I could hear Kael’s ragged breath behind me. “Kael—” I began, but he cut me off, dropping another chip into my palm. “You sold Lily to us,” he spat. “Now it’s just… you and me.”

I stared at the flickering ledger, at the names that shifted with every pulse. My implant throbbed, Lily’s lullaby twisting around my spine like living copper. “If I feed it—what do I become?” I asked.

Ghost’s smile widened, teeth flashing like a corrupted byte. “You become the ledger, the one who decides which stories survive and which are erased.” The torches flared, etching a sigil of interlocking **A** and **X** into the concrete. Their commander raised a gauntleted hand; a synthetic voice bellowed, **ENGAGE.**

Seconds slipped. I could hand the chip to Kael and seal Lily’s echo away—saving a fragment but handing my soul to the void. Or I could let the Engine swallow the ledger whole, risking a cascade that would sync every corporate mind to my frequency, making me the living archive of every secret in Arctos’s depths.

A drop of neon rain slipped through a cracked vent, hitting the ledger and turning the ink to liquid gold. In that instant I saw it all: the rain washing memories away, the ghosts that clung to steel, the contracts that bound them, and a single line that would decide whether the rain would finally bleed out the city—or drown it forever.

**ALERT – 3RD WINDOW.** I lunged for the terminal, data‑spike humming against my palm, and slammed it into the Engine’s core. Code streamed like rain through a gutter; the Engine’s white‑out pulse swallowed the light then burst outward in a cascade of neon diamonds. For a heartbeat everything went black. The next thing I heard was Ghost’s voice, now cracked with static: **“SEE YOU ON THE OTHER SIDE.”**

The vault doors slammed shut, sealing the crew inside. I was alone with the Engine, my implant throbbing a new lullaby—one that sang of a future I didn’t yet understand.

*The rain kept falling.*
