1. **Hook & Stakes** – You’ve set up the inciting incident nicely (email exchange after the conference). If you want to raise stakes early, you could sprinkle a hint of why each character is at a crossroads (e.g., Alex’s burnout with grant deadlines, Maeve’s looming sabbatical decision). 3. **Point‑of‑View Balance** – You’re alternating between Alex and Maeve within the same paragraph sometimes. Make sure each switch is clearly signaled (e.g., a line break, an italicized thought, or a change in verb tense). This helps the reader track whose internal lens they’re viewing.

4. **Pacing of the “chance” moments** – The phrase “the mysterious edits that bound them” implies some kind of editing platform or version control. If you want to foreground that (e.g., a shared Google Doc, a version‑controlled manuscript), naming it can make the connection between “edits” and “bound” feel more concrete.
