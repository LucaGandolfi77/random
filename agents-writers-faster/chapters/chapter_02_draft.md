The rain fell in thin ribbons across the drafting room, each drop a silver nail on the floorboards. Shards of glass, glittering like frozen tears, lay scattered where the orchard‑fruit had exploded, their edges catching the lantern light and throwing it back in shards of memory.

Elisa knelt beside a rain‑slick desk, the gloom of the room hugging her shoulders. Her fingers brushed a drawer half‑closed, and a soft hum rose, as if moonlight itself were breathing inside a glass sphere. She lifted it—a second fruit, whole, its surface a mirror to the storm outside, a pulse of pale light thrumming against her palm.

*"We cannot afford another loss,"* she whispered, half to the fruit, half to the empty chairs around her.

Mara entered, her coat still smelling of ash. In her hand she held a charred manuscript page, the ink smudged into a map of ruin. She flicked the ash away, letting it fall like dying stars.

*"You think you can hide it, Elisa?*" Her voice was a razor hidden in velvet. *"Your fire—your secret—won’t stay hidden when I have proof. Hand over the draft, or I’ll burn the whole orchard to the ground.*"

Elisa’s throat tightened. The ember she had kept hidden in a vial since the first fire crackled faintly against her skin, a reminder of the blaze she had started long ago. She stared at the second fruit, feeling the weight of the grief that the broken shards exhaled—a wave of sorrow that rose from the floor, tasting of smoke and scorched earth.

The shards began to hum, a low, resonant tone that coaxed the scent of the orchard fire back into the room. Small fragments lifted, twirling in the air like fireflies made of glass, each reflecting the raging orchard: bark turning to ember, leaves curling into ash. The agents stood frozen, eyes widened, as the memory of the orchard’s death replayed before them—flames licking the night, a wind of grief that stole breath.

*"It’s… it’s us,"* Mara breathed, her own grief breaking through the hard edge of ambition. *"We all lost something that night.*"

Elisa clenched the hidden ember, feeling its heat seep into her palm. She could melt a shard, silence the echo, but the fire might devour the ancient glass trees that still rooted the orchard’s memory.

She lifted the ember to a particularly bright fragment, and a blue‑flamed flower burst into being, its glass petals shattering outward like crystal snow. The shard sizzled, its memory‑binding glow dimming, the hum fading. But a stray spark jumped, landing on the floor where a tiny bloom of glass began to form, its core pulsing with a captive sunrise.

*"What are you?*" Mara asked, eyes fixed on the newborn flower.

Elisa could not answer. The bloom’s light steadied, casting a new pattern on the walls—an intricate lattice that seemed to write itself in light.

A distant voice, thin as mist, slipped through the cracks of the room, whispering a title no one had yet penned: *"The Unwritten Chapter.*"

The agents stared, the rain still drumming, the glass bloom pulsing, and the future stretched before them like a page yet to be turned.
