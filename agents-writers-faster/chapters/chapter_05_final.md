Mara’s fingers hovered over the edge of the drawer, the same one that had slipped open to reveal the glass fruit on their first night together. She turned to Elisa, her voice a low hiss that curled around the room.

> “You still keep that thing locked?” she demanded, the word sharp as a shard. “It’s the only thing that can keep this story in line. Take it back, Elisa.”

Elisa's eyes flicked to the glossy, deadly glass, and the amber ember at her throat flared, a timid glow against her pale skin. The ink that coated the pages of their manuscript—black as a moonless night—shivered, staining the parchment with impossible undertones of violet.

They stood in the dim glow of scattered candles, a living heart of light and darkness beating in the center of the desk. The ink seeped into the cracks between their fingers, dripping like midnight liquid, staining their skins with a promise of rewriting their pasts.

> “I cannot give it to you,” Elisa whispered, her voice trembling but resolute. “If that fruit were yours, the memory of the orchard would burn again in my mind. I won’t let that happen.”

With a sudden flare, the ember at her throat blew into flame, churning the black ink. The ink swirled around her in an ethereal dance, breaking into violet sparks that erupted like bursts of celestial fire. The room seemed to breathe as the glass fruit's power, once silent, crackled with a new, furious weight.

The ink‑scribe—an apparition wrapped in a garb of soot—stepped forward, ledger in hand. He whispered, his words slithering through the air:.

> “The void has taken our words, but I have a record of what was promised.”

His ledger was a thin page, black cloth that seemed to drink the light. He opened it, showing the ink-stained manuscript morphing—words dissolving into new letters that hissed “forgotten pact.” A ripple ran through the room, each agent catching a different vision of the orchard fire. The memory twisted into a mosaic of truths, a tapestry of doubt.

Mara lunged forward, her fingers clawing at the ledger. The ink‑scribe spun, clutching the book with both hands as Elise’s ember spiraled from her chest into the ink, fracturing the bloom into shards of glass. Light flickered through each shard, dancing across the walls like flickering stars.

The shards pounded the floor; one fell onto the ledger, smudging the ink‑scribe’s last entry. A line new and dark grew amongst the words: 

> *"The void consumes the words."*

The word hovered, a sickly glow, dissolving into the air. The room fell silent, the candle flames guttering as if inhaling it all.

The final shard, a distressed, crystalline cube, hovered above the desk, its facets catching the last glimmer of twilight—ready to decide whether the ink would carry their history forward or erase it entirely.
