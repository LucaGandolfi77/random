When the ledger’s last line blinks, the orchard’s memory cracks open like a wound in the night. 

Mara’s fingers, pale as moon‑silver, pressed the glass shard to the surface of the ledger, its blackened edge pulsing as though it drank sky. The brittle page fluttered, and the ink, twilight‑black, stared back. “It was Lara… who I left behind,” Mara whispered, voice a rasping wind through glass.

Elisa’s heart beat a drum of burnt embers inside her ribs. She felt the ember she had long hidden—her mother’s flame—quake in her chest. With a breath she declared, her voice thin but steel, “Then give it to us. Give her a chance to be remembered.” Her hand clenched over the ember like a torch, the heat leaking to the bloom’s pulse.

The bloom, a living heart of glass, crackled. The chant of its pulse, a low thrum of sorrow, surged and, like the knuckles of a falling storm, a shard exploded outward. It slashed across the parchment, a filament of blue-white light, striking the shared memory etched in the paper among them. The word *fire* splintered, its syllables dissolving into a hollow echo that left a scar of silence between the agents. The ink‑scribe’s eyes, ever faint, glimmered with a violet flare.
