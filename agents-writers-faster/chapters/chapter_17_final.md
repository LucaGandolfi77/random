When the sky cracked, it did so in bruised ink, each vein a razor‑thin seam of blackness that split the horizon. The chorus—in ancient notation, a storm of syllables—whirled above, a tide of sound that turned every spoken line into echo‑phages, devouring the language itself.

Elisa stood between the dance of the ink‑veil and the roiling chorus, her breath a jagged spark of warmth beside the falling syllables. Memory had become a ledger the Quill kept, each stroke a thin line of ink; each line she erased meant a fragment of herself fading. And yet the hymn sang, *Sa‑ri…*, its syllable thinning into the half‑sigil that flickered across the wind.

She reached for the glyph *el‑sì*—a sliver of ink etched into the veil that only appeared when one’s mind slipped beyond the veil’s edge. With a single breath, she spoke the word. The veil convulsed, tears of ink gushing into the sky as the chorus shattered into a static chorus of its own.

The rogue echo‑phage, once a silent menace, erupted in a rush of living, dying sentences that lashed the city’s streets, turning cobblestone into hollow words that swallowed the inhabitants. Elisa felt the last of her memories scream away, replaced by a blank, trembling parchment she now held in her disquieted hand.

The walls of the cathedral dissolved into a mist of syllables, and the ancient keeper whispered a half‑echo of a bargain, its meaning lost in the fractured void. The question hung between them: if she bound the veil and erased herself, would the keeper’s promise still rise—or would her silence be the final song?
