The cathedral dome bled black ink, veins of midnight spider‑webbing across the vaulted stone, pulsing like a heart that had forgotten how to beat.  Elisa stood at its center, the Aetheric Quill trembling in her grip, its feather‑tip dripping with living script.  She drew a breath, each inhale tasting of iron and forgotten lullabies, and pressed the quill to the dome.  The first stroke unfurled, a surge of obsidian that drank the syllable *El‑* from her lips.  The sound cracked, a brittle sigh that fell into the void.

The loss of her name was a physical wound; her throat constricted, a thin line of ash rising with each heartbeat, and an echo of ink flared behind her eyes.  A thin vapour of memory slipped away, leaving a hollow cavity where her identity had lived.  The crack in the dome widened, and from it the Name‑Eater emerged – a jagged, silver‑threaded silhouette that sang a metallic rasp every time a spoken name brushed the air.  Its presence was a vibration, a cold shiver that turned spoken words into razor‑sharp echo‑phages that snatched voices from their owners.

Maeron lunged forward, thrusting a shard of glass he had salvaged from the library’s vaulted cellar.  The shard glittered with a faint, captured sunrise, its surface still humming with the memory of the name it had stolen – Elisa’s lost name, now sealed within the crystal.  "Write," he whispered, and the ink‑etched map on the cathedral walls split into luminous arrows.  Elisa, her throat a dry, echoing cavern, lifted the shard and traced the arrows with a fingertip.  Where the glass met the ink, the Name‑Eater’s rasp faltered, recoiling from the silent script.  The half‑sigil on Elisa’s forearm – a living vein of ink that pulsed in time with the dome’s darkness – flared brighter, each pulse a conduit to the ceiling above.

The silent path led them down spiralling stone stairs, each step a muffled thud swallowed by the veil’s growing black tide.  At the base of the stairs, the crypt breathed a low, ancient thrum – the heartbeat of the keeper hinted at in earlier prophecies.  In its centre floated the Mirror Fruit, a translucent glass orb suspended in a vortex of ink droplets that clung like captured whispers.  Light fractured within it, reflecting not only the stones but also the faint outline of Elisa’s own vanished voice.

The chorus rose, ink tendrils lashing outward, eager to reclaim the orb’s brilliance.  Elisa pressed the Mirror Fruit to her lips; the rim brushed the inside of her throat, and the memory she was about to surrender surged forward: the first vision of the Quill – a silver feather spilling ink across a newborn world, the moment the ink sang its first word.  She could not utter the syllable; her voice was already a sealed tomb.  Instead, the memory poured into the fruit, a cascade of luminescent ink that turned the orb a blinding white.  As the memory vanished from her mind, a phantom taste of ink and ash flooded her senses, and the half‑sigil on her arm ignited, projecting the full sigil across the crypt ceiling in a searing glyph of silver and shadow.

For a suspended heartbeat the veil recoiled, the black ocean retreating like a tide pulled by a moon of forgotten names.  Yet the ancient keeper’s pulse thundered louder, demanding a final price.  Elisa understood – the only thing the veil could not consume was the Quill’s own essence, the core memory of its creation.  She reached deep, recalling the Quill’s first song – the hum that birthed ink, the cadence that set the world’s veins ablaze.  That memory, brighter than any fire, ripped from her soul and fell into the vortex of ink surrounding the Mirror Fruit.  The veil shuddered, then snapped shut, sealing the crypt doorway with a wall of living black glass that reflected nothing but the darkness beyond.

The Name‑Eater dissolved into a sigh of static, its metallic rasp fading into silence.  Elisa stood, voiceless and stripped of the Quill’s first song, the full sigil blazing above her like a promise and a warning.  As the ink receded, a faint, unfamiliar whisper curled from the sealed doorway – *Sa‑ri…* – a half‑remembered name that had never been spoken.  The echo vibrated through the Mirror Fruit, hinting that the missing half of the sigil lingered within the echo‑phage’s memory‑drain, waiting to be reclaimed.

The party stared at the black glass, the Mirror Fruit’s inner light dimming, knowing the veil was held only by the fragments they had sacrificed.  Ahead lay a new hunt – a name spoken nowhere, a memory hidden in the phage’s mouth, and a path that might finally bind the living ink forever.
