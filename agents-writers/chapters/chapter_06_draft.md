### The Veil's Fracture

The market square, once a riot of shouts and clangs, had become a sheet of living ink. A baker’s cry—*twice the price!*—collapsed into a rivulet of black that pooled at his{}{} stall, solid  :

> the sound of the word turned to glass, and the glass swallowed the echo.

A child’s rhyme, a soldier’s command, the mayor’s proclamation—all dissolved the instant they left the mouth, leaving the pedestrians staring at a surface that reflected no sound.  The ink thickened, creeping along the cobbles, turning each footfall into a smear of darkness.

Elisa moved through the tableau, the half‑sigil *—ra* humming against the back of her hand like a dying moth. Every stroke of the Quill she made ripped a jagged line of living ink across the sky, sealing a fissure that threatened to fling the Veil into the heavens. With each stroke the ink seeped into her skin, blooming along her forearms as dark veins that pulsed with a violet light—a cruel reminder that every act of rewriting left a scar on the scribe.

**The first vein**

The first vein tightened around the memory of her mother’s lullaby—*“Sleep, child, the night is soft.”*  The phrase slipped through the fissure, leaving a hollow ringing in her ears. She tried to hum it, but the notes evaporated before they could form. In an instant she was a child again, cradled in her mother’s arms, the lullaby unfinished, then gone. The image shattered, and her heart clenched on a wound that had never healed.

**Ink‑veins and erased fragments**

Each subsequent vein claimed another piece of herself:

* a scar on her left wrist, the mark of the first fire she set in the orchard;
* the name she had called herself since birth; 
* the final line of the Quill{ }  ‘the world begins where ink  meets  thought.’

With every loss the ink‑veins widened, spreading" like veins on a leaf, until the skin at her elbow became a map of darkness.  Her reflection in a shattered pane of ink‑glass was a blur; her face had no edges, her name no longer hung on her tongue.
