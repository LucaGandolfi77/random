Elisa stood at the foot of the cathedral’s obsidian spire, the city below a trembling parchment of glass‑ink. Streets had become translucent sheets, and strangers drifted silently, their names dissolving with every step. The scent of wet ink filled her lungs, heavy as grief, and the pulse of the Veil hammered against her ribs.
