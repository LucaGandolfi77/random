The sky bled black ink, a storm of living script that hammered the moon until its light shattered into shards of obsidian. With each heartbeat the Veil consumed another ten percent of the world’s memory, the air thickening as forgotten names clotted like dried paint on the wind.

Elisa stood at the foot of the cathedral’s obsidian spire, the city below a trembling parchment of glass‑ink. Streets had become translucent sheets, and strangers drifted silently, their names dissolving with every step. The royal guard, once a wall of steel, staggered and fell, their commands erased from mind; the great bridge over the River Lumen turned to ink and collapsed, spilling a tide of blackness into the streets. The scent of wet ink filled her lungs, heavy as grief, and the pulse of the Veil hammered against her ribs.

A glyph pulsed on the stone wall – the echo‑phage’s memory‑drain – a shifting rune that only flickered into view when its bearer hovered on the brink of total oblivion. It shimmered like a dying star, the missing half‑sigil humming beneath its surface, waiting for a sacrifice.

From the shadows a thin veil of light coalesced into the ancient Keeper, its outline half‑caught in the ink‑storm. “The Quill’s origin – the first droplet of ink – is yours, Elisa,” it intoned, each syllable a crack in the Veil. “In return, the House’s recorded history will be erased, every chronicle, every name written in ink will vanish.”
