# The Veil’s Reckoning

The heavens had become a sheet of obsidian ink, rippling with a storm that drank the sun. Each thunder‑clap from the shattered spires of the capital pulsed the Veil outward, spreading its darkness to **eighty percent** of the sky. Below, the streets lay half‑written: cobbles turned to glass, their surfaces slick with living script that leapt up to erase any name that tried to read it.

Elisa stood on the cracked balcony of the ruined library, breath a thin frost in the ink‑filled wind. The half‑sigil burned into her forearm, a jagged black rune that had once promised a binding. Now it throbbed like a wound, a living lock that kept the Quill’s power from spilling completely.

> **Veil spread:** *eighty percent of the sky*.

Behind her, the echo‑phage’s memory‑drain glyph flickered on the stone wall—an ever‑shifting cascade of characters that **only appeared when her thoughts hovered on the edge of total forgetting**. It was a glyph, not a spoken spell; it could be seen but never spoken.

A low, mournful hum rose from the city’s heart, the ancient chorus feeding on forgotten sentences. From the glyph spilled a new threat—a **rogue echo‑phage**, larger than any she had faced. Its body coiled like a serpent of ink‑script, each loop a line of verses that rewrote the very stones it touched. As it slithered, stone turned to blackened glass, bridges’ ironwork softened into ribbons that snapped with a sigh, and the market’s stalls dissolved into unreadable verses.
