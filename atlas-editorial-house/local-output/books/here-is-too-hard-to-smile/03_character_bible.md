# Character Bible

## Main Characters

### Character A (Alex/they/them)
- **Age**: 34
- **Occupation**: Senior product designer at a tech startup
- **Core traits**: Analytical, anxious about obsolescence, perfectionist
- **Motivation**: Escape the cycle of constant redesign and replacement
- **Fear**: Being left behind, irrelevance
- **Love language**: Planning, future-talk, careful consideration
- **Physical details**: Always checking phone for notifications, bites lower lip when thinking
- **Backstory**: Grew up in suburbs, moved to city for career, never quite belongs

### Character B (Morgan/they/them)
- **Age**: 31
- **Occupation**: Freelance graphic designer, teaches evening classes
- **Core traits**: Intuitive, spontaneous, emotionally direct
- **Motivation**: Financial instability balanced by creative freedom
- **Fear**: Not enough, never being secure
- **Love language**: Physical presence, spontaneous gestures, immediate response
- **Physical details**: Always early or late (never on time), sketches in margins
- **Backstory**: Grew up in city, left for college, returned after divorce

### Relationship Arc
- Meet: Chaotic subway transfer, both carrying identical briefcases (mistaken identity)
- Bonding: Shared frustration with city, mutual help
- Tension: Structure vs spontaneity, different fears
- Growth: Learning each other's rhythms, finding balance
- Resolution: Neither fully changes, but accommodates

## Supporting Characters
- **Jordan**: Alex's coworker, becomes Morgan's friend
- **Riley**: Morgan's student, represents youthful hope
- **Casey**: Alex's ex, creates momentary doubt
- **Dr. Liu**: Therapist mentioned but not seen, represents professional help

## Symbolic Motifs
- **Subway tokens**: Lost and found, passing through
- **Coffee cups**: Shared, mismatched, warming hands
- **Sketches**: Alex draws without knowing, Morgan sketches on napkins
- **Clocks**: Always running late or early, time anxiety
