# Revision Strategy

## Quality Gates

### Gate 1: Draft Complete
- Minimum 3000 words verified
- Scene structure follows outline beats
- Character voices distinct and consistent

### Gate 2: Critic Review
- Identify one specific risk per chapter
- Flag thematic inconsistencies
- Note any padding or filler content

### Gate 3: Finisher Pass
- Normalize terminology (ensure names/places consistent)
- Remove padding, tighten prose
- Verify urban atmosphere present

### Gate 4: Continuity Check
- Names, ages, timelines cross-checked
- Symbolic motifs tracked
- City details align across chapters

## Risk Categories (Critic Focus)
1. **Padding Risk**: Content added to reach word count without purpose
2. **Inconsistency Risk**: Character behavior contradicting established traits
3. **Thematic Drift**: Chapter veering from central anxiety/love tension
4. **Cliché Risk**: Falling into romantic-novel tropes
5. **Urban Disconnect**: City as backdrop rather than active element

## Revision Protocol
- Each chapter drafted then immediately reviewed
- No forward progress without critic sign-off
- Finisher passes before next chapter begins
- Translation begins only after all English chapters complete

## Translation Preparation
- Key phrases flagged for cultural adaptation
- Time/day references noted for Italian localization
- Emotional register documented per scene

*This strategy ensures rigorous quality while maintaining creative flow.*
