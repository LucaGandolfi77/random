# Political Inheritance – Project Plan

## Classification
- **Task type**: Literary novel
- **Audience**: Adult readers interested in political drama, family saga, and institutional critique.
- **Primary challenge**: Balancing thematic depth (political inheritance, family obligation, institutional decline) with narrative drive.
- **Risk level**: Moderate – thematic complexity, but no extreme controversy.

## Mode
- **Small Group Mode** – three‑person writers' room to ensure distinct voices without over‑staffing.

## Route (Roles)
| Role | Assigned persona | Rationale |
|------|------------------|-----------|
| Lead | **tolstoy** | Provides structural breadth, historical perspective, and long‑form narrative discipline. |
| Reviewer | **austen** | Ensures readability, character nuance, and that the prose serves the audience. |
| Critic | **poe** | Probes thematic darkness, potential melodrama, and narrative tension. |
| Finisher | **hemingway** | Compresses prose, sharpens clarity, and verifies shipment‑ready language. |

*Note*: Refiner role is covered by the finisher in this modest‑size project.

## Handoff Sequence
1. **Lead → Reviewer** – After drafting each chapter, the lead hands the manuscript to the reviewer with a *Lead‑to‑Reviewer* note containing:
   - Target audience recap
   - Core thematic intent for the chapter
   - Known weak spots (e.g., exposition density)
2. **Reviewer → Critic** – The reviewer annotates structural and stylistic concerns, then forwards to the critic with a *Reviewer‑to‑Critic* brief:
   - Highlighted narrative tension points
   - Questions about thematic resonance
3. **Critic → Finisher** – The critic supplies a *Critic‑to‑Finisher* memo:
   - Main risk or melodramatic element
   - Recommendation for tightening or rebalancing
4. **Finisher → Lead** – The finisher returns the polished chapter with a *Finisher‑to‑Lead* note confirming:
   - Definition of Done (DD) satisfied (theme, length, readability, factual consistency)
   - Final stylistic tweaks applied
5. **Lead integrates** any final suggestions and assembles the complete manuscript for publication.

## Definition of Done (DD)
- **Thematic integrity**: every chapter must address at least one of the three core themes.
- **Length**: 5 chapters, ~12,000‑15,000 words total (≈2,500‑3,000 per chapter).
- **Voice consistency**: unified narrative voice as defined in *Voice Guidance*.
- **Fact‑check**: all historical or procedural references verified (no anachronisms).
- **Polish**: Hemingway’s final pass guarantees readability ≤ grade‑10,‑sentence‑length ≤ 20 words where possible.

## Voice Guidance
- **Narrative tone**: sober, reflective, with occasional lyrical description (rain, architecture) but never overwrought.
- **Point of view**: third‑person limited focused on Eleanor, allowing internal monologue and external observation.
- **Language**: precise diction, avoid archaic flourishes; aim for clarity (Hemingway) while preserving depth (Tolstoy). Use short, impactful sentences for climactic moments; longer, flowing sentences for atmospheric description.
- **Dialogue**: crisp, character‑specific; avoid exposition‑heavy speech.

## Revision Strategy
1. **Lead Draft** – Complete raw chapter adhering to outline.
2. **Reviewer Pass** – Check for pacing, character clarity, and audience engagement; annotate.
3. **Critic Pass** – Identify any thematic dilution, melodrama, or logical gaps; suggest sharpened conflict.
4. **Finisher Pass** – Apply Hemingway compression: cut redundancy, tighten sentences, ensure DD met.
5. **Final Assembly** – Lead incorporates any remaining minor edits, runs spell‑check, and formats markdown for publication.
