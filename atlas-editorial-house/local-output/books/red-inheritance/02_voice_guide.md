# 02_voice_guide.md

## Narrative Perspective
- **POV**: Third-person limited, anchored to Elena Voss for primary narrative arcs
- **Secondary Perspectives**: Allow brief first-person diegetic accounts for intimate secrets or flashbacks

## Sentence Rhythm
- Prioritize long, complex sentences for political discourse and reflective moments
- Use short, punchy sentences during action sequences or emotional confrontations
- Embed lyrical prose in descriptions of decaying estates or historical archives

## Dialogue Policy
- Political speeches should mirror Elizabethan-influenced formal English
- Family conversations maintain modern-English colloquialisms with strategic archaisms
- Bargaining dialogue must use precise, layered metaphors
- Silence as a tool in key emotional scenes (e.g., duel preparations)

## Production Guardrails
1. **Theme Consistency**: Every chapter must advance at least one core theme (power decay, betrayal, institutional decline)
2. **Character Continuity**: Establish recurring motifs (e.g., red symbolism for blood/instability)
3. **Temporal Anchoring**: Night/day cycles must reflect political tension (e.g., night = moral ambiguity)
4. **Risk Integration**: Explicitly name the critic's required risk per chapter

## Revision Process
1. Lead drafts with Tolstoy's 'scope' approach: build world before resolving plots
2. Reviewer (Austen) checks motive alignment with brief
3. Critic (Poe) inserts at least one explicit challenge to emotional logic
4. Refiner (Woolf) ensures interior monologues reflect established psychology

Example: Chapter 01 must show rather than tell Elena's loss of control through environmental details (dust patterns, creaking floorboards) rather than explicit narration.
