# 04_revision_strategy.md

## Structural Revision Pass
1. **Lead Review**: Confirm chapter adheres to chapter outline and maintains forward momentum in the central conflict.
2. **Continuity Scan**: Verify all character relationship states match 03_character_bible.md; flag any discrepancies.
3. **Arc Alignment**: Ensure chapter's thematic contribution advances the primary themes (power decay, betrayal, institutional decline).
4. **Pacing Check**: Assess whether descriptive passages accelerate or stall narrative progression.
5. **Structural Risks**: 
   - melodrama risk: evaluate emotional scenes for overstatement
   - drift risk: verify every subplot connects to the central power struggle
   - unearned resolution risk: ensure conclusions stem from established motivations

## Stylistic Revision Pass
1. **Voice Consistency**: Maintain third-person limited perspective anchored to Elena Voss.
2. **Sentence Rhythm**: Vary between long, complex political discourse and short, punchy action sentences.
3. **Dialogue Authenticity**: 
   - Political speeches use Elizabethan-influenced formal English
   - Family dialogues employ modern colloquialisms with strategic archaisms
   - Bargaining scenes use layered metaphors
4. **Thematic Integration**: Each chapter must embed at least one recurring motif (e.g., red symbolism for blood/instability).
5. **Permanence Guardrails**: 
   - maintain temporal markers aligned with plot turning points
   - preserve symbolic settings (e.g., estate libraries as knowledge repositories)
   - ensure secondary characters speak consistently with their established motivations

## Critic Checklist (to be completed before finisher sign-off)
- [ ] Melodrama: Emotional scenes must avoid gratuitous outbursts
- [ ] Drift: Subplots must converge on the central power struggle
- [ ] Voice Consistency: Narrative tone must remain anchored to Elena Voss
- [ ] Thematic Contribution: Chapter must advance at least one core theme
- [ ] Structural Integrity: No unresolved plot threads left dangling

## Finisher Sign-Off Criteria
1. **Word Count**: Minimum 3,000 words (verified via word processor)
2. **Risk Mitigation**: All identified critic risks addressed through revisions
3. **Terminology Normalization**: Standardize political titles, family registers, and institutional names
4. **Padding Removal**: Eliminate repetitive exposition or descriptive loops
5. **Continuity Confirmation**: Cross‑reference with 03_character_bible.md and 01_outline.md
6. **Thematic Resonance**: Ensure chapter contributes meaningfully to the overall narrative arc
7. **Ship‑Ready Assessment**: Language is compressed, prose is lean, and no further revision is required

## Continuity Archiving
- After each chapter sign‑off, update 03_character_bible.md with any new character developments or relationship shifts.
- Log any canon adjustments in 98_translation_notes.md for later review.
- Record chapter word count and risk assessment in 99_manifest.md.

## Revision Timeline
- **Draft Phase**: 7 days per chapter (Lead writes, Reviewer critiques, Critic surfaces risks, Refiner implements changes).
- **Review Phase**: 2 days per chapter (Finisher conducts final sign‑off, updates documentation).
- **Buffer**: 3 days between chapters for unexpected revisions or continuity corrections.

## Integration With Translation Workflow
- Once English chapter passes finisher sign‑off, the translation pipeline begins.
- 98_translation_notes.md records key decisions regarding:
  - Political language registers (formal vs. colloquial)
  - Family hierarchy terminology (e.g., “uncle,” “cousin,” “heir”)
  - Emotionally charged passages requiring cultural adaptation
- Translator reviews the finisher’s commentary before producing Italian version.
- Translated chapter must meet same 3,000‑word minimum and pass a brief Italian‑language quality gate before final manifest entry.
