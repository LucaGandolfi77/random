+# Novel Planning Document

+**Title (working):** *Inheritance of Shadows*
+
+## Classification
+- **Task type:** Literary novel (book)
+- **Primary challenge:** Structural and thematic depth (political inheritance, family obligation, institutional decline)
+- **Audience:** Adult readers interested in political drama and family sagas
+- **Risk level:** Moderate‑High (complex intergenerational arc, requires careful pacing)
+
+## Mode & Route
+- **Operating mode:** Small Group Mode (lead, reviewer, critic, finisher)
+- **Lead:** `tolstoy` – responsible for overall structure, chapter outlines, and narrative voice.
+- **Reviewer:** `austen` – checks narrative coherence, character consistency, and prose style.
+- **Critic:** `poe` – probes thematic weaknesses, logical gaps, and tonal mismatches.
+- **Finisher:** `hemingway` – compresses, polishes language, ensures clarity and readiness for publication.
+
+## Handoff Sequence
+1. **Lead → Reviewer** – Deliver chapter‑by‑chapter outline with narrative goals, character arcs, and key plot beats. Include open questions needing review.
+2. **Reviewer → Critic** – Provide reviewed outline with notes on prose style, pacing, and any lingering concerns.
+3. **Critic → Lead** – Return a critique highlighting the most dangerous assumptions, structural fragilities, and thematic blind spots.
+4. **Lead → Finisher** – Incorporate reviewer and critic feedback, produce a refined full draft outline and sample chapter prose.
+5. **Finisher → Lead** – Perform final language tightening, verify definition of done, and certify shipment readiness.
+
+## Work Order
+- **Output directory:** `local-output/books/political-inheritance-novel/`
+- **Primary risks:**
+  - Over‑extension of generational timeline.
+  - Inconsistent political context across decades.
+  - Dilution of family‑obligation theme amid institutional decline.
+- **Definition of done:**
+  - Complete chapter‑by‑chapter outline (12 chapters) with ~300‑word synopsis each.
+  - Voice guideline document.
+  - Revision strategy checklist.
+  - All files committed under the above output directory.

The chapter establishes the political climate, family expectations, and the looming inheritance. A young, idealistic grandson, **Milan**, observes the ceremonial transfer of power. |

+| 8 | **Fracture** | Open conflict erupts at a public hearing. Clara defends the institution; Milan testifies against it. The family is split, and the institute’s legitimacy is questioned. |
+| 9 | **Exile and Return** | Milan is forced into temporary exile. He travels abroad, studying alternative governance models, which reshape his vision for reform. |
+| 10 | **Reconstruction** | Returning home, Milan proposes a radical restructuring of the institute, invoking the original covenant’s spirit. He faces both support and betrayal. |
+| 11 | **The Last Inheritance** | A climactic council vote decides the institute’s fate. The decision reflects the culmination of generational obligations and political realities. |
+| 12 | **Legacy** | Epilogue showing the long‑term impact of the decision on the family and the nation, tying together the themes of inheritance, duty, and institutional decline. |
+
+## Voice Guidance
+- **Narrative perspective:** Third‑person limited, alternating between Milan and Clara to contrast youthful idealism and entrenched duty.
+- **Tone:** sober, contemplative, with occasional lyrical passages when describing the institute’s architecture or political philosophy.
+- **Stylistic notes:**
+  - Use **Tolstoy‑style** expansive description for settings and historical context.
+  - Deploy **Austen‑style** wit and precise dialogue for family interactions.
+  - Insert **Poe‑style** atmospheric prose in moments of suspense or revelation.
+  - End each chapter with a **Hemingway‑style** crisp, reflective sentence that hints at the next thematic turn.
+- **Language:** Avoid modern slang; maintain a timeless but accessible register.
+
+## Revision Strategy
+1. **Structural Review (Lead & Reviewer)**
+   - Verify that each chapter advances the central themes.
+   - Ensure the generational timeline is clear and chronologically consistent.
+2. **Thematic Audit (Critic)**
+   - Ask: *Does political inheritance remain foregrounded?* *Is family obligation adequately explored?*
+   - Identify any scenes where institutional decline is merely backdrop.
+3. **Character Arc Consistency (Reviewer)**
+   - Track Milan’s ethical evolution; confirm Clara’s motivations stay believable.
+4. **Fact‑Check Political Context (Fact‑checker – optional)**
+   - Cross‑verify any historical references or political structures with reliable sources.
+5. **Prose Polish (Finisher)**
+   - Trim superfluous adjectives; sharpen dialogue.
+   - Apply Hemingway’s “omit the obvious” principle for clarity.
