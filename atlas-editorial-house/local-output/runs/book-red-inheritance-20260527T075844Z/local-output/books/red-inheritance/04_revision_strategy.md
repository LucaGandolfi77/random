# Revision Strategy for "Red Inheritance"

## Goals
- Ensure narrative coherence and thematic consistency across all chapters.
- Maintain character and timeline continuity.
- Enhance clarity, engagement, and emotional impact.
- Normalize terminology and remove redundant or padded text.
- Prepare for smooth translation to Italian, minimizing cultural or linguistic friction.

## Process
1. **Self-Revision** by Writer: Initial polish focusing on flow and accuracy.
2. **Critic Review:** Identification of narrative risks, thematic gaps, inconsistencies, pacing issues.
3. **Writer Revisions:** Address critic feedback, deepen character motivations where needed.
4. **Finisher Pass:** Language polish, tightening prose, terminology normalization.
5. **Final Review:** Cross-check continuity, verify minimum word count (3,000+ words), and completeness.
6. **Translation Preparation:** Note key cultural or emotional nuances for adaptation guidance.

## Tools
- Version control for iterations.
- Document comparison tools for tracking changes.

## Roles
- Critic: Must name at least one specific risk upon chapter sign-off.
- Finisher: Ensures consistency, polish, and removal of padding.

## Timeline
- Revision cycles follow each chapter's drafting phase before translation.
