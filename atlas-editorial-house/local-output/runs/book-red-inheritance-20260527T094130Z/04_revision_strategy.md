# Revision Strategy for "Red Inheritance"

This document outlines the comprehensive revision strategy for each chapter of the book "Red Inheritance." The strategy ensures rigor in critique, refinement, review, and finishing phases to produce a polished, coherent novel.

---

## Revision Workflow

### 1. Critique Phase
- **Objective:** Identify weaknesses, inconsistencies, and risks in the chapter.
- **Process:**
  - Read the chapter fully.
  - Annotate areas needing improvement or clarification.
  - Identify and list named risks specific to this chapter (e.g., plot holes, unclear motivations).
  - Note any terminology inconsistency and padding (wordiness or redundant sections).

### 2. Refinement Phase
- **Objective:** Address critique points and enhance chapter quality.
- **Process:**
  - Rewrite or adjust sections flagged during critique.
  - Normalize terminology throughout the chapter for consistency.
  - Remove padding to meet the minimum word count of 3000 words with meaningful content.
  - Confirm character names, timeline events, institutions, and theme alignment.

### 3. Review Phase
- **Objective:** Validate revisions and check for continuity and coherence.
- **Process:**
  - Reviewer reads the revised chapter from a fresh perspective.
  - Verify all critique concerns are addressed.
  - Check chapter word count (minimum 3000 meaningful words).
  - Perform continuity checks across character names, timelines, institutions, and overarching themes.
  - Reviewer documents any outstanding issues or approves the chapter.

### 4. Finishing Phase
- **Objective:** Final polish and prepare the chapter for sequencing in the manuscript.
- **Process:**
  - Fix any last spelling, grammar, or style issues.
  - Apply any stylistic or voice adjustments to fit the whole book.
  - Confirm no padding remains and terminology is fully normalized.
  - Ensure the chapter feels integrated into the novel as a coherent part of a whole.

---

## Roles and Responsibilities

### Critics
- Conduct detailed critique of draft chapters.
- Identify named risks and problem areas.
- Provide actionable feedback for refinement.

### Refiners
- Execute revisions based on critique.
- Enhance clarity, flow, and terminology consistency.
- Remove padding without sacrificing essential content.

### Reviewers
- Verify that refinements meet standards.
- Check for continuity throughout the novel.
- Approve chapters or request further revision.

### Finishers
- Perform final editing and polishing tasks.
- Ensure textual and thematic integration in the full manuscript.
- Confirm chapter readiness for final manuscript.

---

## Chapter Completion Criteria
- Chapter contains at least 3000 words of meaningful, coherent text.
- All named risks for the chapter have been resolved or clearly documented.
- Terminology is normalized across the chapter and consistent with the manuscript.
- Padding is completely removed; text is clear and concise.
- Continuity of character names, timelines, institutions, and themes is verified.
- The chapter is approved by reviewers and finished as per criteria.

---

## Continuity Checks
- Confirm consistent character names and traits.
- Validate timeline sequencing and event logic.
- Verify institutional names and functions remain unchanged or evolve logically.
- Ensure themes are consistent and developed progressively.

---

This revision strategy is designed to create a seamless, professional-quality novel that maintains integrity and engagement from start to finish.
