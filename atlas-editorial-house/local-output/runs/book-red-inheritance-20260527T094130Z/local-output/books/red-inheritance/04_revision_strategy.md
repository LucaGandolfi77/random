# Red Inheritance - Revision Strategy

## Overview
This document outlines the process for critique, refinement, review, and finishing quality assurance for each chapter of "Red Inheritance."

## Roles and Responsibilities
- **Critic:** Reviews each chapter, identifying at least one specific risk related to plot, character continuity, thematic clarity, or pacing.
- **Refiner:** Works with writer to address critic's feedback, improving narrative coherence and depth.
- **Reviewer:** Validates that the chapter meets minimum criteria of 3000 words and follows voice and thematic guidelines.
- **Finisher:** Normalizes terminology, removes padding, ensures tight, polished prose.

## Chapter Completion Criteria
- Completed draft of at least 3000 words.
- At least one named risk identified and addressed.
- Terminology and character naming consistent with character bible.
- Padding, redundancy, and verbosity removed.
- Chapter fits coherently within overall novel arc, maintaining continuity across characters, timelines, institutions, and symbolic motifs.

## Process Steps
1. Writer submits draft chapter.
2. Critic reviews and provides risks and suggestions.
3. Writer and Refiner revise draft accordingly.
4. Reviewer confirms compliance with word count, voice, and thematic rules.
5. Finisher applies final polishing.
6. Chapter marked complete and file saved.

---

Maintaining these rigorous standards ensures a coherent, polished novel aligned with project goals.
