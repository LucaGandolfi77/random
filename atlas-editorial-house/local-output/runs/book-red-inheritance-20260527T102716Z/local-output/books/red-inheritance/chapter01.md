Victor Caruso stood by the window, his silhouette rigid against the sprawling city that lay beyond, a kingdom of power and decay.

The red inheritance that had defined his family was not merely a matter of wealth or status; it was a legacy steeped in blood, betrayal, and the unyielding pursuit of dominance. This chapter opens with Victor's internal struggle—balancing the weight of his patriarchal responsibilities with the encroaching doubts about the institution he had helped build.

We meet Elena, his wife, whose graceful exterior masks a mind sharp with calculation. Their marriage is a complex dance of loyalty and ambition, a cornerstone of the family's fragile unity. The arrival of their eldest son, Marco, at the estate signals a shift. Marco's ambitions are palpable, and his presence brings both hope for continuity and the threat of upheaval.

As the day unfolds, the tension within the Caruso household simmers beneath polite conversation and carefully crafted smiles. The undercurrent of suspicion is palpable: alliances are tested, old wounds reopen, and the first shadows of betrayal begin to lengthen.

Outside the estate, the political landscape is shifting. Institutions that once upheld the Caruso family's power show signs of erosion, with rival factions maneuvering in the shadows. Alessandro Rossi, a charismatic and ruthless politician, emerges as a formidable adversary, setting the stage for conflict both public and private.
