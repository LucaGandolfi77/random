Cemetery Chorus

Mercy Bell

Haunted

I am Mercy Bell, speaking now from under vinegar and weather.
They thought they knew me by the work I did as a nurse, but my real trade was keeping count of losses.
Mercy Bell died in autumn when work, worry, and weather finally agreed against the body.
They praised my usefulness; they were really rewarding how well I concealed reserve.
The truth is this: I have long suspected that Silas Vale, not any Bell, gave me my face, and I hated the suspicion because it felt too much like belonging to the wrong room.
Ask Thomas Quill what silence cost us.
Bury me beside a hand kept steady; let the town call that justice if it needs a pretty word.

Cross references: Gideon Bell, Thomas Quill

Frank Harrow

Wistful

I am Frank Harrow, speaking now from under dust and weather.
They thought they knew me by the work I did as a child, but my real trade was keeping count of losses.
Frank Harrow was born, and every old loyalty in Morrowfield adjusted itself around the cradle.
They praised my steadiness; they were really rewarding how well I concealed nothing they could prove.
The truth arrived late and had to share a room with pride.
Ask Arthur Harrow what silence cost us.
Bury me beside a kept promise; let the town call that justice if it needs a pretty word.

Cross references: Arthur Harrow, Caleb Vale, Miriam Harrow

Jonah Harrow

Haunted

The truth is this: I drew relief money into the mill and let gratitude stand in for wages because power was easier to keep than fairness.

Ada Quill

Haunted

I am Ada Quill, speaking now from under glass light and weather.
They thought they knew me by the work I did as a bartender, but my real trade was keeping count of losses.
Ada Quill died in summer when work, worry, and weather finally agreed against the body.
They praised my wit; they were really rewarding how well I concealed keeping everybody's secrets.
The truth is this: I kept Gideon Bell's letters to the singer from Galena behind the brandy shelf because holiness deserves at least one witness who can laugh.
Ask Thomas Quill what silence cost us.
Bury me beside midnight laughter; let the town call that justice if it needs a pretty word.

Cross references: Thomas Quill
