Cemetery Chorus

Mercy Bell

Lyrical, Intimate, Ironic, Humane, Compressed

I kept the winter lamp lit while bandage met skin and pulse answered after the room went quiet; usefulness bloomed like frost, and I called it love. Thomas Quill held the basin without spectacle, and I kept that hush longer than any vow. Gideon Bell kept the face the town expected, and I carried Mercy Bell into town memory—autumn at last agreed with the body, and I, who feared dependency and gossip, let care count me beyond myself, close enough to be wanted, far enough to remain a stranger to my own sleep. I confused deserving with being needed, and the confusion, sweet and ruinous, outlived me like a small, steady pulse in the cold.

Ruth Vale

Wry Solemnity

: I, Ruth Vale, stood as a bell of office rung from beneath old water, counting affection like rent because once it cost too much. In autumn, work, worry, and weather finally agreed against my body, and I spoke aloud what the town had only guessed.

  • Morrowfield 1901: Contracts and LanternsMercy Bell was carried into town memory as autumn pressed work, worry, and weather against the body, while Caleb Vale and Miriam Harrow turned careful courtship into a civic marriage under public scrutiny. Arthur Harrow arrived carrying several families at once, and every old loyalty in Morrowfield bent around the cradle, even as Silas Vale renewed a family bitterness against Jonah Harrow. The church staged Harvest Supper where compliments sounded like contracts, the town hall reopened credit and appetite at the Thaw Market, Bell Night made widows and liars sing together at the cemetery, and the Lantern Regatta let the river look forgiving for a night.
